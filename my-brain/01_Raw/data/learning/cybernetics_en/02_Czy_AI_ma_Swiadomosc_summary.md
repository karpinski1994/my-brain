# Summary: Cybernetics #2 - Does AI Have Consciousness?

## Key Concepts
*   **Joules vs. Bits:** The Industrial Revolution was the "Revolution of Joules" (amplifying physical energy via steam engines). The current AI era is the "Revolution of Bits" (amplifying cognitive processing via correlators).
*   **AI as Information Algorithms:** Cybernetics rejects the marketing term "Artificial Intelligence," preferring "Information Algorithms" (Algorytmy Informacyjne). AI is simply a programmed steering process that converts input into output.
*   **The Correlator and Talent:** The human brain is a correlator. Talent is defined by the physical density and structure of neural connections we are born with. 

## Consciousness and Autonomy
*   **Does AI have consciousness?** Cybernetically, yes. Marian Mazur defines consciousness simply as "pathways of correlative conductivity" (the ability to link inputs to outputs based on stored codes). 
*   **Does AI have free will (Transcendence)?** No. AI cannot step outside its programming to spontaneously generate its own autonomous goals.
*   **Will AI take over the world?** No. AI lacks a "Homeostat" (the cybernetic organ that defends a system's functional equilibrium and independence). AI is just a tool, like a hammer. The danger lies in the *humans* using the tool, not the tool itself.

## The Human Advantage
*   **Diagnostic Power:** The human brain's theoretical capacity (10,000 connections ^ 80 billion neurons) vastly outpaces current AI, running on a mere ~20 watts of energy.
*   **Conclusion:** We should not fear AI. We should use it to offload tedious cognitive tasks, allowing us to focus entirely on cultivating our unique, unrepeatable human talents.
