## What is Dynamism of Character?
While the previous episode focused on the rigid parameters of the *Correlator* (informational track), this episode focuses on the rigid parameters of the *Accumulator* (energo-material track). The **Dynamism of Character** describes how a person's available disposable power changes over the course of their lifetime — and this change is predictable, mathematically describable, and completely outside of individual control. It is a rigid parameter.

**Key ratio:** Dynamism = the relationship between the *rate of biological growth/development* and the *rate of material entropy* (degradation of the body's material quality over time). Since entropy always moves in one direction (quality can only decrease), the body's strategic response is to rapidly build up in youth and then strategically slow down and conserve in old age.

## The Five Stages of Life

Professor Mazur defined five stages of character dynamism:

### 1. Exodynamism (Egzodynamizm) — Rapid Growth
The body is growing rapidly. The quality of biological material is still very high, so energy is abundant and regenerates quickly. Characteristic behavior: high energy, fast recovery, risk-taking, play, exploration, not thinking about the future. *Example: children who can run until they collapse, sleep it off, and be fully recharged the next day.*

### 2. Exostatism (Egzostatyzm) — Slowing Growth
The body approaches its optimal peak. Energy is still plentiful and regenerates, but the person begins to notice that planning and organizing the environment pays off. Early goal-setting capacity begins to emerge. *Best time for starting a family, having children, and launching long-term projects.*

### 3. Statism (Statyzm) — Equilibrium
The body has reached its peak. The primary drive is *balance* — what you give must return to you. People in statism want stability, order, and fairness in exchanges. They are excellent at maintaining and optimizing established processes. *Potential conflict with exodynamics: a parent in statism who wants a clean house clashes naturally with a child in exodynamism who wants to scatter energy everywhere.*

### 4. Endostatism (Endostatyzm) — Slowing Decline
The body begins to decline. The person notices that tomorrow they will have *less* energy than today. A new strategy emerges: leveraging *other people's* disposable power. The person naturally begins to develop social influence — giving direction, setting goals, organizing others to act — so that their goals are realized through the combined energy of those around them. *This is the natural phase for mentorship, leadership, and institutional work.*

### 5. Endodynamism (Endodynamizm) — Accelerating Decline
The body is in a pronounced decline phase. Disposable power is significantly reduced. However, the endodynamic has an extraordinary gift: the ability to think in **timescales longer than a single human lifetime**. They can set goals for processes that will only complete long after they are gone. *This corresponds to wise elders who share distilled life wisdom, and who organize multi-generational strategies.*

## The Intergenerational System
The most natural and effective human social unit is the **multi-generational family**. Each dynamism phase complements the others:
*   The exodynamic grandchild brings physical energy to help the endodynamic grandparent.
*   The endodynamic grandparent brings wisdom, structure, and direction to the exodynamic grandchild (a "transgression" of play — combining physical energy with higher-level cognition via guided tasks).

Breaking up multi-generational families (a pattern seen in modern urban/Western society) is, from a cybernetic perspective, a **form of deliberate societal sabotage** that reduces the efficiency of humanity's collective self-steering.

## Practical Applications

### Optimal Age for Having Children
*   **Women:** First child between 18–25 (exostatism) — the mother has abundant energy and knows tomorrow she will have even more. She can sustain high-energy engagement with the child.
*   **Men:** First child between 20–35 (exostatism–statism) — the father can engage creatively and physically with young children.

Having children later (in statism or endostatism) creates a **conflict of dynamisms**: a statism parent wants order and predictability; an exodynamic child needs chaos and energy expenditure. This creates unnecessary friction that is hardwired into the biology of both parties.

### Team Composition
A well-designed team covers all phases of dynamism:
*   **Exodynamics:** Innovation, new ideas, physical energy for implementation, visible "face" of the team.
*   **Statics:** Maintenance, standardization, process quality, deep focus within their talent zone.
*   **Endostatics:** Direction, goal-setting, social organization, long-term vision, mobilizing others' energy toward the shared goal.
