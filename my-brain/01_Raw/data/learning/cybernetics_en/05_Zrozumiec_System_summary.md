# Summary: Cybernetics #5 - Understanding the System

## Key Concepts
*   **System vs. Process:** A system is a static snapshot (a single film frame) that exists to transform external stimuli into a reaction. A process is the continuous change of that system over time (the movie reel).
*   **The Anatomy of a System:** Systems operate on two parallel tracks:
    1.  **Informational Track:** *Receptor* (gathers input) -> *Correlator* (processes information).
    2.  **Energo-Material Track:** *Supplier* (gathers energy/matter) -> *Accumulator* (stores/processes energy).
    *   **Effector:** The shared output organ that combines energy and information to emit a reaction back into the environment.

## The Role of the Homeostat
*   **Defining the Goal:** A system needs a reason to exist. The *Homeostat* is the organ that dictates the system's ultimate goal and maintains its equilibrium against external threats.
*   **Business Application:** In a company, the owner/founder is the homeostat. They define *why* the business exists. The CEO/managers are simply the correlators executing the logic to achieve that goal. Therefore, founders should focus on being the homeostat (working *on* the business) rather than getting bogged down as a correlator (working *in* the business).
