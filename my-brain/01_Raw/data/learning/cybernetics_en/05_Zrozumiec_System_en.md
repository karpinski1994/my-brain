# Cybernetics #5: Understanding the System - The Key to Conscious Steering

**Speakers:** Marcin Kapusta and Zenon Dominik Dudek

## What is a System?
In this episode, the conversation dives into one of the most fundamental concepts of cybernetics: the **System**.

A **system** exists to transform something in its environment into something that did not exist before. Every **system** has a boundary that separates it from its environment. The fundamental purpose of any **system** is to emit a **reaction** (output) into the environment in response to a **stimulus** (input/bodziec).

- **Business Example:** A manufacturing company is a **system**. The stimuli are raw materials, energy, and information entering the factory. The reaction is the finished product delivered to the market.

## System vs. Process
A common point of confusion is the difference between a **system** and a **process**.
- **System:** A snapshot at a specific moment in time. Think of it as a single frame on a reel of film.
- **Process:** The change of a **system** over time. Think of it as the entire movie playing out. When a company reacts to the market (e.g., releasing a hit product), the market changes, which in turn provides new stimuli to the company, forcing the company to adapt. This continuous evolution is a **process**.

## The Anatomy of a System
In the physical (energo-material) and abstract (informational) world, a cybernetic **system** consists of distinct functional organs divided into two tracks.

### 1. The Informational Track (Processing Bits)
- **Receptor:** The organ that receives stimuli from the environment and turns them into impressions/information.
  - *Human:* Eyes, ears, touch.
  - *Machine:* Microphone, camera, weight sensor.
- **Correlator (Korelator):** The organ that processes the information gathered by the **Receptor**. It connects internal codes with external reality. Importantly, a **Correlator** does not have to be in one place (e.g., human decisions are made in the brain, but also via the nervous system or gut).

### 2. The Energo-Material Track (Processing Joules)
- **Supplier (Zasilacz):** The organ that draws energy and matter from the environment into the **system**.
- **Accumulator:** The organ that processes and stores this energy/matter within the **system** until it is needed.

### 3. The Output (Shared by both tracks)
- **Effector:** The organ that takes the processed information and the stored energy and executes the **system**'s reaction back into the environment.
  - *Human:* Hands gesturing, vocal cords speaking.
  - *Machine:* A speaker turning digital code back into sound waves.

## The Homeostat: The Core of Purpose
If a **system** has **Receptors**, **Correlators**, **Accumulators**, and **Effectors**, it can function—but *why* does it function? This is where the **Homeostat** comes in.

The **Homeostat** is the functional organ that defines the **system**'s **goal**. It maintains the **system**'s internal equilibrium and defends the **system** against external disruptions that might prevent it from reaching its goal. It is the "regulator of regulators."

- **In a Human:** The **Homeostat** is highly decentralized but is most closely tied to the hormonal **system**, which regulates biological survival. Philosophically, it is the element that connects the physical body and the abstract mind (what some might call the "soul").
- **In a Business:** The true **Homeostat** is the **owner** or **founder**. The owner defines *why* the company exists. A CEO or manager might run the day-to-day operations (acting as the **Correlator**), but the owner ensures the company stays true to its fundamental purpose.

This cybernetic truth perfectly explains the famous business adage: **"Work *on* your business, not *in* your business."** When an entrepreneur works *in* the business, they are just a **Correlator**. When they work *on* the business, they are fulfilling their true role as the **Homeostat**.
