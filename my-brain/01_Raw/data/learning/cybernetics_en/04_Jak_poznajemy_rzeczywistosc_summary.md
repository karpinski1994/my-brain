# Summary: Cybernetics #4 - How Do We Perceive Reality?

## Key Concepts
*   **The Three Domains of Cognition:** Professor Marian Mazur divided human cognition into three distinct, complementary levels: Art, Philosophy, and Science.
*   **Art (Sztuka):** Based purely on *Assumptions* and *Impressions*. It is the broadest form of cognition, dealing with raw emotional and sensory input without needing to explain or prove it.
*   **Philosophy (Filozofia):** Based on Assumptions + *Theorems*. It builds structures, explanations, and relations around our impressions but lacks a rigorous method of proof.
*   **Science (Nauka):** Based on Assumptions + Theorems + *Proof Procedures*. It is the strictest domain, requiring repeatable, falsifiable methods to prove that a claim aligns with reality (empirically or abstractly/mathematically).

## Practical Applications
*   **Identifying the Domain:** In modern discourse, media, and business, people often present emotional impressions (Art) or unproven theories (Philosophy) as if they were objective facts (Science).
*   **Improving Communication:** Before arguing, establish shared assumptions. If someone makes a claim, ask for their proof procedure. If they don't have one, acknowledge that the conversation is in the realm of Philosophy, not Science.
*   **Combating Information Overload:** As information overwhelms society, people regress to making decisions based on raw emotional impressions (Art). Using this cybernetic categorization helps restore logical structure and sanity to decision-making.
