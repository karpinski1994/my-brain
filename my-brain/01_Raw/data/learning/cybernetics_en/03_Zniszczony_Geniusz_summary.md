# Summary: Cybernetics #3 - The Destroyed Genius & The Student Who Predicted the Future

## Key Concepts
*   **The Marginalization of Marian Mazur:** The founder of the Polish Cybernetics School, Marian Mazur, was ostracized by the communist academic establishment because he refused to play political games. His work was plagiarized, but his genius was validated when NASA hired him to use psychocybernetics to predict astronaut group dynamics for space missions.
*   **Józef Kossecki & Sociocybernetics:** Mazur's student, Docent Józef Kossecki, took cybernetics into the realm of geopolitics. He created a highly accurate model for predicting international relations. Before his death in 2015, he accurately predicted the modern geopolitical shifts involving the US, China, Russia, and Ukraine.
*   **The General Theory of Motivation:** After Kossecki's death, the National Information Academy (NAI) completed the "General Theory of Motivation." It evolved Kossecki's sociological models into a strict, mathematically sound deductive axiom, providing an objective framework for understanding human and societal motivations.
*   **Information vs. Code:** 
    *   **Information:** Connects elements within the same set.
    *   **Code:** Connects elements across different sets. 
    *   In modern business, the greatest friction is a lack of unified semantic **code** (people using different definitions for the same words). Cybernetics provides a strict structural language to eliminate this communication breakdown, massively increasing organizational efficiency.
