# Summary: Cybernetics #6 - Steerable, Self-Steering, and Autonomous Systems

## Key Concepts
Cybernetics categorizes **systems** based on the location and role of the **Organizer** (the entity that defines the **system**'s goal).

1.  **Steerable Systems (Systemy Sterowne):**
    *   *Organizer Location:* External.
    *   *Behavior:* The **system** has no internal decision-making capacity. It only functions when directly manipulated by the **Organizer** (e.g., a flashlight turned on by a human, or a micro-managed business where the owner does everything).
2.  **Self-Steering Systems (Systemy Samosterowne):**
    *   *Organizer Location:* External.
    *   *Behavior:* The **system** can manage its own internal operations and adapt to minor changes, but it does so to achieve a goal dictated by an external force (e.g., a colony managed by local elites for the benefit of an empire, or corporate departments executing a CEO's strategy).
3.  **Autonomous Systems (Systemy Autonomiczne):**
    *   *Organizer Location:* Internal (called the **Homeostat**).
    *   *Behavior:* The **system** defines its own goals and actively defends its ability to exist and steer against external threats (e.g., a human being, or a highly evolved, self-sustaining organization with unified internal goals).

## Practical Application
*   **The Power of AI:** The current AI revolution allows individuals and businesses to quickly transition from exhausting "Steerable" workflows (doing everything manually) to highly efficient "Self-Steering" workflows. By offloading routine cognitive tasks to AI **Correlators**, humans can elevate their role to that of the pure **Homeostat**—focusing exclusively on setting goals, long-term strategy, and adapting to major environmental shifts.
