# Summary: Cybernetics #14 - Understand Yourself and Your Role in the Matrix of 12 Motivations

## Key Concepts
- The control process has **6 sub-processes**: Exploration, Classification, Explication (cognitive) and Postulation, Optimization, Realization (decision)
- Each of the 6 splits into two based on origin, yielding **12 total motivation types**
- **Decision motivations** split by Internal/External: Ideological vs Adaptive postulation, Ethical vs Legal optimization, Vital vs Economic realization
- **Cognitive motivations** split by Meta/Sub: looking up to larger systems vs looking down into subsystems
- The **matrix of decision roles** maps people by whether they postulate, optimize, and/or realize: Investor, Entrepreneur, Cooperator, Solo Entrepreneur, Advisor, Manager, Worker, and Supervisory Board (cognitive role)
- **Kosecki's logic gate** (bramka logiczna) formally connects cognitive input (System X) with decision output (Goal Y) via a method/operation
- An **autonomous process system** requires all 12 motivation types to be present — removing any one breaks its autonomy
- Humanity (8 billion people) is the autonomous process system where each person contributes different motivational talents, collectively filling all 12 slots

## Key Insights
- Confusing goals with methods is a common steering error, historically conditioned in Poland by external organizers to suppress strategic thinking
- Cognitive and decision motivations are like **two legs** — you need both to stand, walk, and function effectively; most organizations overvalue decision-makers and overlook cognitive roles
- Understanding your own dominant motivations (e.g., adaptive postulation vs ideological postulation) lets you understand how you relate to reality and what drives you
- A person who only reacts externally or only acts internally is not autonomous — true autonomy requires balanced internal and external motivation across all 12 types
- The 12-type framework is both rigorous ("dangerous" — remove one and the system fails) and hopeful (8 billion unique people means countless ways to fill each motivational function)
- Individual happiness correlates with the happiness of others — even self-interest, properly iterated through the control process, leads to altruistic conclusions
