# Summary: Cybernetics #10 - School Destroys Your Talent: Rigid Parameters of Character

## Key Concepts
*   **Character (Cybernetic):** The *set of rigid (fixed) steering properties* of an autonomous system. Not a moral judgment — a technical term for the immutable structural properties that define how a system reacts.
*   **Rigid vs. Elastic Parameters:**
    *   *Rigid:* Unchangeable, biologically fixed (e.g., number of limbs, brain architecture, talent).
    *   *Elastic:* Modifiable through learning and experience (e.g., languages, skills, habits).
*   **Three Rigid Parameters of the Correlator:**
    1. *Intelligence:* Quantity of correlative nodes (neurons) — raw capacity.
    2. *Aptitude:* Speed of forming connections — processing speed.
    3. *Talent:* The uneven *distribution* of dense connection clusters across different domains — the "highways" of the brain.
*   **Core Insight:** Every person has unique talent in some area that cannot be replicated elsewhere with the same efficiency. Forcing people to be average in all areas (standard education) prevents the full expression of individual talent and reduces Free Power.
*   **IQ Critique:** IQ tests mix all three parameters in unknown proportions and measure only a narrow slice of talent, making them poor and often misleading diagnostics of true cognitive capacity.
*   **Better Tool:** Cybernetic Resonance (Rezonans Cybernetyczny) test — measures character parameters relative to the Mazur-Kossecki theoretical framework.
