# Summary: Cybernetics #12 - Why Does the World Follow the Old? — Breadth of Character

## Key Concepts
- **Breadth of character (L) = Tolerance (T) + Susceptibility (M/podatność)** — the total range of environmental conditions a person can handle
- **Tolerance (T):** the range of deviations from ideal conditions a person can accept without changing themselves (like engineering tolerance — acceptable deviation from the norm)
- **Susceptibility (M):** the ability to change oneself to adapt to the environment — requires expending working power (e.g., putting on a coat when cold)
- Tolerance and susceptibility are **independent parameters** — they can vary separately
- The **"saddle" graph:** breadth of character (L) over lifetime — starts high at conception, drops steeply to a minimum around age 17–25, then rises again towards death
- **Four character types:** narrow/unsusceptible, narrow/susceptible, broad/unsusceptible, broad/susceptible
- **Freedom violated** when a person is deprived of stimuli within their tolerance range
- **Dignity violated** when a person is forced to receive stimuli outside their tolerance range

## Key Insights
- At birth: **high susceptibility, low tolerance** — a child must adapt to survive but can't tolerate deviations
- At ~17–25: **minimal breadth of character** — peak rebellion age because young people won't tolerate much and can't easily be forced; they want to shape the world to their vision
- In old age: **high tolerance, low susceptibility** — the elderly accept much but won't change themselves or their goals
- Old commanders (endodynamics) are **stable goal-setters** — they don't waver but may tolerate massive losses; best when goals align with those they lead
- Young commanders (exodynamics) are **good in frontline combat** — they expend energy freely — but poor at long-term resource management and strategy
- The **modern battlefield** is intergenerational, interdisciplinary, and informational — requiring precision over speed, favoring endodynamic leaders
- **Victory** (per Dominik): when the opponent unknowingly starts realizing your interests — introducing your program into their control system
- **Steering susceptibility:** use coercion (law, norms) or encouragement (ethics)
- **Steering tolerance:** change the environment or reframe the goal — don't try to change the person directly
- Practical example — **football training:** kids who love football are within tolerance; kids forced to play are in susceptibility range; over-formalizing the sport can destroy a child's love for it by pushing it outside their tolerance
- The **"you can't teach an old dog"** principle: young people can learn new skills (high plasticity), the elderly cannot (low susceptibility)
- **Parents of teenagers (17–25):** the child's narrow breadth of character means they'll rebel and tolerate little — parents must consciously tolerate the behavior to maintain connection, not try to suppress it
- **Grandparents** traditionally filled this gap — as endodynamics with high tolerance and low susceptibility — but the nuclear family model has removed that support, contributing to family breakdown and youth seeking compensation in subcultures, stimulants, or the internet
