# Cybernetics #8: Free Power and the Accumulator - How Systems (and People) Store Energy

**Speakers:** Marcin Kapusta and Zenon Dominik Dudek

## What is the Accumulator?
The **Accumulator** is the functional organ of the energo-material track responsible for gathering, storing, and distributing potential (energy). Its two core jobs are:
1. **Storing energy for the Effector** — providing the power necessary to carry out the system's physical reactions into the environment.
2. **Supplying all internal processes** — providing the energy needed to run the Receptor, Correlator, and Homeostat internally.

**In the human body:** Blood is an excellent analogy for the accumulator. It gathers oxygen, glucose, and nutrients, then distributes them to whatever organ needs them at any given moment — whether a muscle needs to contract, or the brain needs to process a decision.

Like the Correlator, the Accumulator has three rigid structural parameters: the *quantity* of its matter/substrate, the *quality* of that matter, and the *conductivity* between its elements.

## The Three Types of Power (Moc)
Professor Mazur defined a crucial hierarchy of power, which reveals the true economics of any system—from a human body to a nation:

### 1. Idle Power (Moc Jałowa)
The minimum energy a system must spend *just to exist and maintain itself* in time. For a human, this is heartbeat, body temperature, cell regeneration, and breathing. A company's "idle power" is rent, payroll, and basic operational costs needed just to keep the doors open. **This is not optional — it is the cost of existence.**

### 2. Work Power (Moc Robocza)
The energy required to *obtain more idle power* from the environment. For a human, it's the effort of going to work to earn money to buy food to stay alive. For a company, it's all the productive activity needed to generate revenue. **Taxes are classified as work power — they are a cost imposed on the system to obtain permission to continue existing.**

### 3. Free Power (Moc Swobodna)
**The most important concept in this episode.** Free Power is the *surplus* energy remaining after Idle Power and Work Power are covered. It is the only power a system can use to actually *steer itself* — to set goals, optimize, grow, invest, and adapt the environment to its own parameters.

*   **For a Human:** Free Power is your time and energy left after meeting your basic survival needs and the effort required to earn that survival. It's the power of hobbies, self-development, relationships, art, and strategic planning.
*   **For a Business:** Free Power is profit margin that can be reinvested into growth, innovation, or rewarding the owner.
*   **Cybernetic Definition of Happiness:** Mazur defines happiness as the *alignment of a system's character parameters with its environment*. Free Power is the mechanism that makes this possible — you need it to actually change your environment to match yourself.

## Power as Geopolitics: The Real Game
The concept of Free Power reveals the underlying logic of historical and modern power dynamics:

*   **Slavery/Feudalism:** The most direct form of power extraction. The master ensures the slave/serf's entire productive capacity (Work Power + Free Power) is consumed, leaving zero Free Power to plan an escape or resistance.
*   **Modern Taxation/Cost-of-Living:** A more sophisticated version — by controlling the prices of energy, land, food, and water, and layering taxes on top, an external organizer can systematically convert what would be a citizen's Free Power into additional Work Power, eliminating the ability to self-steer.
*   **China's Rare Earth Strategy:** A real-world geopolitical example of applying Free Power logic. By restricting rare earth exports, China forced all other nations dependent on these materials to significantly increase their Work Power (the effort required to obtain energy/materials), effectively reducing their global Free Power and steering capacity.

## Accumulator and Homeostat Communication
The Accumulator communicates with the Homeostat (the goal organ) through two signals:
*   **Load/Stress (Naprężenie):** A signal that energy reserves are being depleted. The Homeostat responds to restore equilibrium (e.g., triggering hunger).
*   **Overload/Relaxation (Odciążenie):** A signal that energy storage is being restored. The Homeostat responds to confirm equilibrium.
