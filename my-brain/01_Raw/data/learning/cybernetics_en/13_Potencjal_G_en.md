## The Psyche From a Cybernetic Perspective

Most people treat the word "psyche" as a primitive concept — something they vaguely imagine but cannot define. In Marian Mazur's cybernetics, the psyche is given a precise definition: it is the area of information interactions, specifically the informational track of an autonomic system.

More precisely, Mazur defines the psyche as all the relations between the Correlator and the Homeostat within the information track. This includes everything from receptor stimulus through sensation, all the interactions in the Correlator, feedback through the Homeostat, and ultimately the output reaction via effectors. However, connections from the Homeostat to the Accumulator are not part of the psyche — those belong to the energo-material track.

The chapter on psyche is Chapter 10 in Mazur's "Cybernetyka i Charakter" (1976).

## Potential G and K — Conductivity and Power

The discussion focuses on a specific diagram showing two coupled variables: **G** (conductivity / przewodność) and **K** (correlation power / moc korelacyjna).

Think of it like electrical wiring:
- **G (conductivity)** is like the copper cable itself — the neural pathways, the structure
- **K (correlation power)** is like the electrical current flowing through that cable — the actual energy

Having wiring installed (G) doesn't mean a light bulb will turn on. You need current (K) flowing through it. Both must coexist.

The process works like this:
1. A receptor potential (Vr) enters the Correlator
2. The distribution of correlation conductivity (G) within the Correlator determines how the correlation power (K) flows
3. This power flow produces an output — either an **estimative potential** (Ve — responsible for decisions) or a **perturbation potential** (Vp — output toward the Homeostats)
4. When the estimative potential crosses the decision threshold, a signal is sent to the effector and a purposeful reaction occurs

The Homeostat represents the "free will" component — it determines what becomes estimative and what becomes perturbative. Without the Homeostat potential, there would be only a correlation process where the system can only sense whether it has power or not. The Homeostat enables purposeful linking of the information track (structure) with available power (energy) to transform that structure.

## G-Potential and Neural Pathway Formation

A key insight: correlation processes build the very pathways they use.

Using the example of a baby learning to squeeze its fist:
1. A stimulus creates a receptor potential in the baby's hand
2. This power in the receptor potential starts building correlation conductivity pathways — like using electricity to build the cable it flows through
3. With repetition, reflex pathways form (muscle memory)
4. The Correlator can then execute the movement with less power, using the reflex path without conscious Homeostat involvement
5. This is the most efficient state — minimal loss in both correlation and energo-material terms

The G-Potential represents this accumulated conductivity. Every repeated action strengthens the pathway (G increases), making future executions more efficient.

## Energy Source for the Correlator

At the abstract model level, Mazur treats the correlation process as having no losses — the power needed in the Correlator simply exists because it's needed. But in concrete reality (an actual human), the Accumulator powers all correlation processes because the entire organism is one integrated system.

A helpful visualization: the development of a zebrafish embryo shows a main energo-material channel from which the entire neural structure is built. Energy, matter, information, and code all originate from the same point.

## The Three Paths of Correlation

Within G and K, Mazur identified three distinct pathways:
1. **Reflexes** — conditioned and unconditioned (odruchy)
2. **Consciousness** — świadomość
3. **Imagination** — wyobrażenia

These three paths operate through the same G and K machinery but at different levels of Homeostat involvement.

## The Stages of Control

Mazur divided all control (steering) into two fundamental processes:

### Cognitive Process (Poznawczy) — determining "what IS"
1. **Exploration** — establishing that something exists
2. **Classification** — identifying what the elements are
3. **Explication** — understanding how the elements connect to form a functional whole

### Decision Process (Decyzyjny) — determining "what SHOULD BE"
1. **Postulation** — defining the goal/target image
2. **Optimization** — choosing the method to achieve it
3. **Realization** — securing and applying the resources

These six sub-processes are logical conjunctions — if any one is missing, the whole process yields zero. Every person must have all six, but their strengths vary.

## The Six Motivation Types

From the six control stages, six corresponding motivation types emerge:

| Motivation | Control Stage | Description |
|------------|--------------|-------------|
| Exploration | Cognitive 1 | Motivated to discover what exists |
| Classification | Cognitive 2 | Motivated to categorize and organize |
| Explication | Cognitive 3 | Motivated to understand how things connect |
| Postulation | Decision 1 | Motivated to set goals and vision |
| Optimization | Decision 2 | Motivated to find the best method |
| Realization | Decision 3 | Motivated to execute and gather resources |

Someone with strong decision motivations but weak cognitive motivations might build utopias — beautiful visions disconnected from reality. Someone with strong cognitive motivations but weak decision motivations might understand everything but never act.

## Strategy, Tactics, and Operations

The discussion extends the control stages into military/management terminology:

- **Strategy** = Postulation (goal-setting at the ideological/environmental intersection)
- **Tactics** = Realization (the resources used)
- **Operations** = Optimization (the methods applied)

A key insight: these levels must remain ordered. What many call "strategy" is often just operational or tactical activity.

## The Goal of Motivation

At the highest level, all motivation ties back to one thing: **happiness**. In cybernetic terms, happiness is defined as "the alignment of character parameters with the environment." The DNA program determines what environment is optimal for each person, and they will be motivated to transform their environment to achieve that alignment.
