## A Critical Note on Definitions
Before discussing "character," the speakers emphasize that Mazur's use of the word *character* is a technical term—a convention—not the everyday meaning ("he has good/bad character"). The cybernetic definition of character has nothing to do with morality or personality. Mazur chose this word from the Polish vocabulary to label a specific scientific phenomenon, just as physicists chose the word "force" to describe something precise.

> *"It is not the word 'character' that matters, but rather what we designate by convention with this term."*  
> — Marian Mazur

This precision is the best defense against manipulation: when your terms have exact definitions, it is much harder for external organizers to replace the meaning of words with something that serves their interests.

## What is Character? (Cybernetic Definition)
**Character** is the set of *rigid (fixed) steering properties* of an autonomous system.

Among all the properties of a system, some are *steering properties*—they directly influence how the system reacts to stimuli from its environment. Among *steering properties*, some are **rigid** (unchangeable, not dependent on the environment or learning) and some are **elastic** (can be changed through experience, learning, etc.).

**Character = the rigid steering properties only.**

### Rigid vs. Elastic Parameters
*   **Elastic (non-rigid):** Things we can change — knowledge of languages, dance skills, expertise in a domain, habits.
*   **Rigid:** Things we cannot change — the number of hands, the structure of the heart, the core architecture of our correlator.

## The Three Rigid Parameters of the Correlator (The Informational Track)
Professor Mazur identified three rigid parameters characterizing the correlative (informational) architecture of a human:

### 1. Intelligence (Inteligencja)
The *quantity* of correlative nodes (registrants/neurons). This is the total possible capacity for storing information/associations. The brain has approximately 80 billion neurons; since information = a relation between two elements (not just one element), the theoretical capacity is 2^(80 billion). This is an astronomically large number, meaning all humans have an enormous ceiling — the practical differences between people are in the *structure* of connections, not the raw number.

### 2. Aptitude / Processing Speed (Pojętność)
The *speed* of creating connections between nodes — how quickly the brain can form new associations. This determines how quickly a person "grasps" new concepts and processes incoming information.

### 3. Talent
The *distribution* of nodes and connection density across different domains. Talent is the structural "architecture" of the brain — where the "highways" of dense, efficient connections exist versus where only "dirt roads" exist.

**Talent is both fixed and unequal.** The brain's physical space is limited; dense clusters of neurons in one area mean less density elsewhere. Therefore, everyone has some areas where they are naturally efficient (their talent) and other areas where they must work much harder for lesser results.

## The Key Insight: You Cannot Fight Talent
A human being will always naturally tend toward their area of talent — the paths of highest conductivity, least effort, and most satisfaction. Attempting to force someone to excel in areas outside their talent (as the standard educational system does by enforcing uniformity) is not just inefficient — it actively prevents the development of genuine uniqueness.

The educational and socio-political systems that push everyone toward the average are, from a cybernetic perspective, not errors in design — they are often intentional **infowiruses** that prevent individuals from discovering and leveraging their unique talent, thereby making them more manageable and keeping their Free Power low.

## IQ Testing: A Flawed Metric
IQ tests conflate all three rigid parameters (intelligence, aptitude, and a slice of talent) in unknown proportions, and test only a narrow band of talent (logical-mathematical reasoning). The result is a pseudomeasure that:
*   Does not accurately reflect total cognitive capacity.
*   Does not reveal what an individual's *unique* talent actually is.
*   Is primarily useful for *ranking* people on a narrow scale, not for *understanding* their full steering potential.

A much more useful diagnostic tool is the **Cybernetic Resonance (Rezonans Cybernetyczny)** test developed by Maciej Bryczkowski, which measures character parameters in reference to the Mazur-Kossecki framework and allows comparing people on an objective, deductive scale.
