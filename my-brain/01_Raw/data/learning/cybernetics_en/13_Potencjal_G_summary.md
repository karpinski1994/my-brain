# Summary: Cybernetics #13 - G-Potential and the Factors of Psyche and Motivation

## Key Concepts

- **Psyche (cybernetic definition):** The area of information interactions in an autonomic system — all relations between the Correlator and Homeostat within the information track. Not the energo-material track.
- **G (Correlation Conductivity):** Like a copper cable — the neural pathways and structure through which power can flow. Built through repetition.
- **K (Correlation Power):** Like electrical current — the actual energy flowing through the G pathways. Both G and K must coexist for any cognitive process to occur.
- **G-Potential:** The accumulated conductivity of neural pathways. Every repeated action strengthens it, making future executions more efficient with less energy.
- **Three correlation paths:** All cognitive/decision processing runs through one of three pathways: Reflexes, Consciousness, or Imagination.

## The Six Stages of Control

| Stage | Process | Question Answered |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| Exploration | Cognitive | Does something exist? |
| Classification | Cognitive | What elements are there? |
| Explication | Cognitive | How are they connected? |
| Postulation | Decision | What should the goal be? |
| Optimization | Decision | What method should I use? |
| Realization | Decision | What resources do I need? |

All six are logical conjunctions — you cannot skip any. Everyone has all six, but their relative strength varies per person.

## The Six Motivation Types

Each control stage produces a corresponding motivation type:
- **Exploration motivation** — driven to discover
- **Classification motivation** — driven to categorize
- **Explication motivation** — driven to understand connections
- **Postulation motivation** — driven to set vision/goals
- **Optimization motivation** — driven to find the best method
- **Realization motivation** — driven to execute and gather resources

## Strategy vs Tactics vs Operations

- **Strategy** = Postulation (goal-setting — the intersection of idea and environment)
- **Tactics** = Realization (the resources deployed)
- **Operations** = Optimization (the methods applied)

These map to military command levels: strategic (what), operational (how), tactical (with what).

## Key Insights

- A person with strong decision motivations but weak cognitive motivations builds utopias — visions disconnected from objective reality.
- A person with strong cognitive motivations but weak decision motivations understands everything but never acts.
- The ultimate driver of all motivation is **happiness** — defined cybernetically as the alignment of character parameters with the environment.
- The Homeostat represents free will in this model — it determines what becomes estimative (decision-driving) and what becomes perturbative.
- The Accumulator powers all correlation processes in concrete reality, though the abstract Mazur model treats the correlator as having no losses.
