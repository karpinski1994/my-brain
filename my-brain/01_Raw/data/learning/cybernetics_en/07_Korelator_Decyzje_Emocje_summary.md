# Summary: Cybernetics #7 - The Correlator: Decisions and Emotions

## Key Concepts
*   **Correlator:** The functional organ responsible for processing information and codes. In humans, it corresponds to the brain + nervous system + hormonal system. Its outputs are **decisions** (actions) and **emotions** (signals to the Homeostat about system equilibrium).
*   **Correlator Structure:** Four internal potentials: Receptor (sensory input), Homeostat (reflective input from internal goals), Estimatory (decision output), Perturbatory (emotional/disturbance output).
*   **Rigid Parameters (Fixed at Birth):**
    1. *Intelligence:* The quantity of correlative nodes (neurons).
    2. *Aptitude/Processing Speed:* The speed of information flow between nodes.
    3. *Talent:* The uneven distribution—the "highways" of densely connected nodes in specific domains.
*   **Cybernetic Happiness:** Using your unique talent in an environment that responds positively to it.
*   **The IQ Myth:** Standard IQ tests conflate intelligence, aptitude, and a narrow slice of talent in unknown proportions, making them poor and potentially misleading measures of a person's full capacity.

## Infowiruses and Normowiruses
*   **Infovirus:** A conditioned stimulus-response link injected via media/culture that triggers a reflex reaction *before* rational thought. Operates between external stimulus and early internal state.
*   **Normovirus:** A deeper infection that alters *internal* rules of thumb—reflexes, imagination, and consciousness—shaping what a person considers normal, possible, or desirable.
*   **Socialization** is a packet of normowiruses. Healthy socialization develops talent; predatory socialization (used by colonial powers and manipulative institutions) redirects a person's energy away from their talent to serve an external organizer's goals.
