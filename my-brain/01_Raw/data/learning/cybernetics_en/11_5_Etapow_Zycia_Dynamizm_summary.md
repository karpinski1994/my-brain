# Summary: Cybernetics #11 - 5 Stages of Life and the Dynamism of Character

## Key Concepts
*   **Dynamism of Character:** A rigid parameter linked to the Accumulator. It describes the predictable, mathematically-describable change in a person's disposable power (Free Power + Work Power) over the course of their lifetime. It is beyond individual control.
*   **Core Driver:** Entropy. The quality of biological material peaks at conception and decreases throughout life. The body's strategic response shifts from rapid growth (to maximize while quality is high) to careful conservation (to maximize lifespan).

## The Five Stages

| Stage | Polish | Energy Level | Primary Drive |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Exodynamism | Egzodynamizm | Very High / Fast Recharge | Play, exploration, no future thinking |
| 2. Exostatism | Egzostatyzm | High / Still Regenerating | Begin organizing environment, early goal-setting |
| 3. Statism | Statyzm | Stable, Peak | Balance, fairness, order, process optimization |
| 4. Endostatism | Endostatyzm | Declining | Social leverage — organizing others toward your goals |
| 5. Endodynamism | Endodynamizm | Significantly Reduced | Multi-generational strategy, wisdom transmission |

## Key Insights
*   **Multi-generational families** are the optimal social unit: each dynamism phase is complementary. Dismantling extended families reduces collective self-steering efficiency.
*   **Optimal family formation timing:** Women: 18–25 (exostatism); Men: 20–35 (exostatism/statism). Having children in statism/endostatism creates structural conflict between the parent's need for order and the child's need for energy expenditure.
*   **Team design:** Intentionally mix dynamism phases — exodynamics for innovation/energy, statics for process maintenance, endostatics for direction-setting and social leverage.
