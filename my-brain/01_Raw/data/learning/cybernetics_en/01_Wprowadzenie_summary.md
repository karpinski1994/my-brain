# Summary: Cybernetics #1 - Introduction

## Key Concepts
*   **Definition of Cybernetics:** The science of steering. More specifically, steering is defined as "purposeful interaction" (acting upon a system with a specific future goal in mind).
*   **Physics vs. Cybernetics:** Physics traditionally analyzes past causes to explain present states. Cybernetics envisions a future state (a goal) and determines the present actions required to achieve it.

## Historical Timeline
*   **Ancient Greece:** The origin of the term (*kybernetes* / helmsman). It was initially about steering ships and managing states/societies.
*   **Roman Empire:** Applied cybernetic principles to warfare and logistics. Example: Inventing sidewalk curbs so soldiers could march by feel, conserving mental energy.
*   **World War I:** The turning point that required cybernetics to re-emerge. The invention of airplanes meant anti-aircraft artillery had to predict future trajectories in 3D space, which was too complex for traditional linear calculations.
*   **Polish Cybernetics School:** Spearheaded by Professor Marian Mazur. 
    *   His early work was destroyed in the 1944 Warsaw Uprising.
    *   Post-WWII communist censorship banned cybernetics until the military realized its necessity in the 1960s.
    *   Mazur developed the "Qualitative Theory of Information" and unified scientific language, treating cybernetics as a meta-language to prevent miscommunication across different scientific disciplines.
