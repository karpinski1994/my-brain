The sources describe a wide range of narratives, ranging from psychological maps used in professional copywriting to structural frameworks for literature, film, and business strategy.
Copywriting and Sales Narratives
These narratives are designed as psychological maps to guide readers from curiosity to a final action, such as a purchase
.
StoryBrand: Positions the customer as the hero who has a problem and encounters your brand as a guide who provides a plan and calls them to action
.
BAB (Before–After–Bridge): A transformation-focused storytelling method that paints a picture of a current relatable problem (Before), illustrates a desirable future where the problem is solved (After), and presents the product as the path between them (Bridge)
.
PASTOR: A comprehensive narrative framework covering the person and Problem, Amplifying the consequences, sharing a relatable Story, describing the Transformation or providing Testimonials, presenting the Offer, and requesting a Response
.
Star-Story-Solution: Introduces a relatable main character (the Star), tells an engaging Story of their struggle, and explains how they emerged victorious through the Solution
.
4Ps (Picture, Promise, Prove, Push): Uses a vivid Picture to engage the reader's imagination, makes a bold Promise of benefits, provides Proof through data or testimonials, and gives a clear Push for action
.
AICPBSAWN: A detailed formula including Attention, Interest, Credibility, Proof, Benefits, Scarcity, Action, Warning of consequences, and a call to act Now
.
Classic and Cultural Narrative Structures
These frameworks represent the "scaffolding" beneath memorable novels and films
.
Hero’s Journey (Monomyth): A quest-based narrative where a protagonist leaves their ordinary world, faces tests and ordeals, and returns home fundamentally transformed
.
Dan Harmon’s Story Circle: A simplified 8-step circular journey (You, Need, Go, Search, Find, Take, Return, Change) that focuses heavily on character growth and internal change
.
Kishōtenketsu: A traditional East Asian four-act structure (Introduction, Development, Twist, Conclusion) that does not rely on conflict but instead generates interest through discovery and unexpected perspective shifts
.
Three-Act Structure: The classic dramatic model consisting of Setup, Confrontation, and Resolution
.
Five-Act Structure (Freytag’s Pyramid): A standard dramatic pattern including Exposition, Rising Action, Climax, Falling Action, and a Resolution or Catastrophe
.
Fichtean Curve: A narrative structure that skips exposition and drops characters directly into a series of escalating crises leading to a major climax
.
In Medias Res: A technique where the narrative starts "in the middle of things," dropping the reader into intense action before filling in gaps through flashbacks
.
Technical and Planning Narratives
Snowflake Method: A planning narrative that iteratively expands a single-sentence summary into a full-length novel outline through ten defined steps
.
Seven-Point Structure: Focuses on seven pivotal signposts: Hook, Plot Point 1, Pinch Point 1, Midpoint, Pinch Point 2, Plot Point 2, and Resolution
.
Save the Cat Beat Sheet: A highly structured roadmap of 15 bite-size beats designed to ensure emotional highs and lows are distributed evenly
.
22 Building Blocks (John Truby): A detailed "checklist" of ingredients for founding a story world and protagonist journey
.
Frame Story: A narrative device where a main overarching narrative surrounds one or more embedded stories
.
Nonlinear Narrative: A story that break chronological flow entirely, jumping between past, present, and future through flashbacks or fragmented scenes
.
Professional and Strategy Narratives
SCQA (Situation–Complication–Question–Answer): A logical structure for business briefings where clarity is prioritized over drama
.
Pixar’s Story Spine: A "fill-in-the-blank" template used for quick storyboarding that emphasizes cause-and-effect actions
.
Jobs-to-be-Done Story: Frames a narrative around a triggering event, a job the user wants to accomplish, and the desired outcome
.
Data Storytelling: A pattern used for research and analytics that follows a Hook → Metric Shift → Why → Implication → Decision flow
.
