[00:17:52] **The Hero's Journey Framework**
The best story structure is the Hero's Journey, which you've seen in movies like *Rocky Balboa*.
It's a story of transformation from rags to riches, overcoming obstacles, and having epiphany
moments. For our purposes, we can simplify this framework:
1. **The Problem:** Start with the struggle. "I struggled with learning algorithms and felt like I
was too stupid to get my dream job." This builds credibility and empathy.
2. **The Journey/Struggle:** Describe the different things you tried that didn't work. This shows
you've been through it all.
3. **The Turning Point (Epiphany):** This is where you introduce your method or solution.
"Then I found out how to use SEO to market my LinkedIn profile, and offers started flowing in."
4. **The Result/Transformation:** Show how your life changed after implementing the solution.
"Now I no longer waste so much time sending emails because this app I built does it for me, and
I can spend time with my family."
5. **The Call to Action (CTA):** End by inviting them to take the next step. "If you want to know
more, I'm offering a no-obligation, risk-free consultation. Just comment 'interested' or DM me."
[00:24:28] **A Simpler Structure and Strong Hooks**
An even simpler structure is:
1. **Strong Hook:** A headline that makes them stop scrolling.
2. **Problem/Pain:** Describe the problem using the story framework.
3. **Solution/Turning Point:** Share your tip or concept.
4. **Call to Action:** Tell them what to do next.
A strong hook should be problem-focused, emotional, ultra-specific, result-oriented, and
curiosity-driven. For example: "How a programmer can get a salary of $15,000 a month in 90
days without learning hundreds of algorithms."
[00:28:33] **Writing the Body and Call to Action**
When writing the body of your post, keep it real, emotional, and simple. Use short sentences
and small details like numbers to make it more believable. The goal is to make the reader think,
"That's me! That's my problem."

Even with a logical, technical audience, stories work because they follow a logical

Before you post, think about the potential objections your audience might have and handle them
