This 30-day onboarding plan is designed to transition you from a state of "anxiety-driven procrastination" to a state of "Gameful Automaticity." Drawing on the Octalysis framework and your psychological portrait, this plan focuses on reaching the "Win-State" of your onboarding phase: feeling competent, confident, and ready to engage with your business for the long term
.
Week 1: The "Tutorial" & Environment Design
Goal: Reduce activation energy and overcome the "friction of newness" using temporary Black Hat scaffolding
.
Days 1–3: Physical and Digital Armor.
The LoL Trap: Place a physical post-it note over your TV/Monitor power button or your League of Legends shortcut that says "Entry Cost: 5-Minute Caption Polish"
.
Digital Boundary: Set up Forest or Cold Turkey to block LoL-related sites and streams during your "Quest Window" (e.g., 9:00 AM – 11:00 AM)
.
Days 4–7: The "First Strike" Buff.
Establish a "nongenotiable" 15-minute ritual at 9:00 AM
.
Task: Write exactly one sentence for a LinkedIn post or edit 1 minute of video subtitles.
Reward: Completing this first thing in the morning grants a "First Strike" XP Multiplier (2x) for that task [Conversation History, 273].
Week 2: Building Momentum & "Progress Visualization"
Goal: Transition from fear-based motivation to Core Drive 2: Development & Accomplishment
.
Days 8–10: Implementing Weighted XP.
Since subtitle editing is high-resistance for you, assign it 3x XP compared to tasks you enjoy [Conversation History, 272].
Use a progress bar (in Notion or a spreadsheet) that fills up visibly as you complete these "boring" segments
.
Days 11–14: Narrative Reframing.
Stop calling it "marketing." Rename your work blocks to "Documenting the Guild’s Progress"
.
Instead of "writing post," your quest is: "Decoding a coding struggle for the junior resistance"
. This shifts focus from "Self" (fear of judgment) to "Them" (helping others)
.
Week 3: Creative Empowerment & Variable Rewards
Goal: Prevent boredom and "routine decay" using Core Drive 3 (Creativity) and Core Drive 7 (Unpredictability)
.
Days 15–18: The D20 Strategy.
To make dull tasks exciting, use a Variable Reward system. After completing a video segment, roll a 20-sided die
.
Roll 1-15: Standard XP.
Roll 16-19: Bonus "Dopamine Dollar" to spend in your rewards shop (e.g., ordering takeout)
.
Roll 20: "Legendary Reward" – A guilt-free, 2-hour League of Legends session [Conversation History].
Days 19–21: Speedrunning the Flow.
Artificially increase the challenge of subtitle editing to enter a Flow state. Set a timer for 10 minutes and try to beat your personal record for "Subtitles per Minute" [Conversation History].
Week 4: Identity Reinforcement & "Boss Battles"
Goal: Solidify your identity as a "Guild Leader/Architect" rather than a "Slave to the Business"
.
Days 22–25: High-Stakes Accountability.
If procrastination returns, use stickK to create a "Commitment Contract." Put a small financial stake on the line that goes to an "anti-charity" if you don't post to LinkedIn 3 times this week
.
Days 26–28: The First "Boss Battle."
The Boss: Posting a video testimonial or a controversial opinion piece on LinkedIn
.
Strategy: Use the "Just One" technique. Commit to just clicking the "Post" button and immediately closing the browser to avoid checking for comments for 4 hours
.
Day 30: Level Up Ceremony.
Conduct a "Monthly Patch Notes" review. Total your XP, see your progress, and award yourself a "Major Reward" (e.g., new tech gear or a trip) to celebrate graduating from the onboarding phase
.
Summary of Daily Loops
Morning Buff: High-protein breakfast (No simple carbs)
.
The Quest: 30–60 minutes of "Documenting the Journey" (Marketing/Editing)
.
XP Logging: Immediate visual feedback of your level growing
.
Reward Window: Spending earned XP on gaming or relaxation only after the daily quest is cleared
.
