Setting up a rewards shop is a critical step in turning your productivity system into a sustainable "human-focused" game. The fundamental principle is to establish a strict daily exchange of productivity and reward: if you want to do something indulgent, you must complete a "sucky" task first
.
To set up your shop, follow these steps:
1. Establish Your Currency (XP Economy)
Before you can "buy" rewards, you must define how much your time and effort are worth. Points should reflect cognitive resistance and actual impact rather than mere volume
.
Weighting for Boredom: Since subtitle editing is high-resistance for you, it should grant significantly more XP (e.g., 3x more) than tasks you find easy [Conversation History, 726].
Quest Milestone Values: A common model assigns values based on frequency:
Daily Quests: 1 Point
.
Weekly Quests: 2 Points
.
One-Off Projects: 15–25 Points (scaled by complexity)
.
Dopamine Multipliers: Implement "buffs" like doubling points for tasks completed immediately upon waking to build momentum
.
2. Structure Your Tiers
Divide your rewards into three tiers to ensure you always have access to quick wins while saving for major milestones
:
Minor Rewards (Low XP Cost): Immediate, small comforts to sustain daily momentum. These might cost 15 XP
.
Examples: 5–15 of rule-free spending cash, a favorite snack, or a 10-minute break
.
Moderate Rewards (Medium XP Cost): Leisure activities that take more time.
Examples: A 45-minute nap, watching one episode of a TV show, or ordering takeout
.
Major Rewards (High XP Cost): Long-term purchases linked to significant achievements.
Examples: Concert tickets, specialized gear (like a custom dice set), or travel arrangements
.
3. Budget for Sustainability
The most common mistake is over-promising rewards you cannot afford. You must budget your rewards against your actual projected spending to keep the system realistic
. For example, if you assign a dollar value to a "Dopamine Dollar," ensure that completing your weekly tasks doesn't exceed your actual discretionary budget
.
4. Special Mechanics for High-Stress Periods
Since you have a tendency to escape into League of Legends when overwhelmed, your rewards shop can act as a "Dopamine Trap" filter:
The Gaming Cost: Instead of gaming for free, make a 2-hour session a "Legendary Reward" that must be purchased with XP or unlocked via a random D20 roll after work is finished [Conversation History, 123].
Randomized Rewards: To keep dull tasks interesting (Core Drive 7: Unpredictability), use a Dopamine Menu with a randomizer. Completing a task could grant you a roll for a surprise reward, which prevents routine decay
.
5. Implementation Tools
You can manage your shop using several platforms:
Notion: Use templates like "Life Game Mode" to track your XP, coins, and rewards in one dashboard
.
Spreadsheets: Use a "Dopamine Dollars" or "Pixel Quest" sheet to automatically tally earnings and display your "bank account" for rewards
.
Habitica: Use the built-in "Rewards" column to list both in-game gear and custom real-life rewards (like "Watch 1 episode of Netflix") that you purchase with in-game gold
.
