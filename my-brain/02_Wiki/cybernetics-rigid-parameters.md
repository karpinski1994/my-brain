# Cybernetics: Rigid Parameters of Character

The immutable structural properties of an autonomous system — intelligence, aptitude, talent, dynamism, and breadth of character.

## Summary

Character (cybernetic) = the set of rigid, fixed steering properties. Unlike elastic parameters (skills, languages) which can be modified, rigid parameters are biologically fixed. Every person has unique talent that cannot be replicated elsewhere with the same efficiency.

## Three Rigid Parameters of the Correlator

1. **Intelligence**: Quantity of correlative nodes (neurons) — raw capacity
2. **Aptitude**: Speed of forming connections — processing speed
3. **Talent**: Uneven distribution of dense connection "highways" across domains

## IQ Critique

IQ tests mix all three parameters in unknown proportions and measure only a narrow slice of talent. Poor and misleading diagnostics. Better tool: **Cybernetic Resonance test** (Rezonans Cybernetyczny) based on Mazur-Kossecki framework.

## Five Stages of Life (Dynamism of Character)

| Stage | Energy Level | Primary Drive |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| Exodynamism | Very High | Play, exploration, no future thinking |
| Exostatism | High | Begin organizing, early goal-setting |
| Statism | Stable, Peak | Balance, fairness, order |
| Endostatism | Declining | Social leverage, organizing others |
| Endodynamism | Significantly Reduced | Multi-generational strategy, wisdom |

## Breadth of Character (L)

L = Tolerance (T) + Susceptibility (M)

- **Tolerance**: Range of deviations from ideal conditions a person can accept without changing
- **Susceptibility**: Ability to change oneself to adapt to environment
- "Saddle" graph: breadth starts high at birth → minimum at ~17-25 → rises again toward death

### Four Character Types
1. Narrow + Unsusceptible
2. Narrow + Susceptible
3. Broad + Unsusceptible
4. Broad + Susceptible

## Key Insight

Forcing people to be average in all areas (standard education) prevents full expression of individual talent and reduces Free Power. The optimal strategy: identify and develop unique talent, don't try to fix "weaknesses."
