# LinkedIn Growth & Algorithm

## Summary

LinkedIn growth is driven by connection mechanics, engagement reciprocity, and understanding the algorithm's preference for expert-led content. Raw follower count matters less than quality connections and engagement.

## Connection Mechanics

LinkedIn operates on a connection-to-connection basis:
- **1st connections** — directly connected; your post is initially shown to this pool
- **2nd connections** — connected to your 1st connections; see your post only if 1st connections engage
- **3rd connections** — furthest reach

If 1st connections engage, LinkedIn pushes your post to 2nd and 3rd degree audiences. This means you need active, interested 1st connections.

### NIPA Method for Finding Connections
1. Go to an active creator/founder in your niche
2. Look at their comment section for people matching your ICP
3. Check their headlines for identification
4. Open profiles in new tabs (10-20 at a time)
5. Engage with their content, then send personalized connection requests

### Daily Connection Target
Add 10-50 people daily. Majority should be potential clients; the rest should be people who have your potential clients in their network.

### Digital Detox (Starting from Zero)
Remove irrelevant connections (old classmates, former colleagues) that fill your feed with noise. Option: start a brand new account for a clean slate.

## Commenting for Growth

Commenting is the highest-leverage activity when starting from zero.

### Strategy
- Find 20-30 active accounts in your niche (mix of 0-5K, 5-20K, 20K+ followers)
- List their URL and posting time
- Comment as early as possible on their posts
- Target: 20-30 comments daily minimum

### What to Comment
- A personal take on the post topic
- A motivational mic-drop (quote matching the tone)
- A meme or humor (pattern interrupt)
- **Never**: "Great post", AI-generated comments, single emojis

### Why It Works
Each comment is seen by the author AND their audience. If you comment on 10 accounts with 10K followers each, your comment reaches up to 100K eyeballs. Top comments (high likes) get pushed further.

## Algorithm Mechanics

### 2026 Shift
LinkedIn is moving from rewarding quantity to rewarding expertise. The algorithm now hunts for domain authorities — people who can prove they've done what they teach.

Key signals:
- Expert-to-expert engagement triggers algorithmic amplification
- Posts from verified experts get more reach than generic advice
- Likes are no longer the metric that matters — qualified leads are

### Time Zone Hacking
LinkedIn sends notifications when you repost a post. Reposting 6 hours later can increase engagement by up to 20% by reaching a different time zone's audience.

### Scheduling Decreases Reach
Scheduling tools create an extra automation layer LinkedIn can detect. Testing shows engagement drops by ~10% on scheduled posts. Scheduled posts also remove you from the critical first-hour engagement window.

### Shadow Ban Recovery
1. Go to your most recent post
2. Message 5-10 commenters, thanking them
3. Engage with new active people in your feed
4. Connect with new people — this signals activity to LinkedIn

### Posting Routine
Block 12:00-1:30 PM daily. Warm up by commenting 12:00-12:30. Post at 12:30. Reply to every comment in the first hour. Use remaining time to engage more.

## Authority Building

### Strategic Authority Stacking
- Macro: create content highlighting client work and results
- Micro: highlight social proof across your entire profile
- Feature in publications, work with known brands, get testimonials

### Authority Bias
Mentioning specific numbers, companies, and accomplishments triggers authority bias. Leads with proof, then promise, then delivery mechanism.

### Expert-Led Engagement System
Comments should add expert-level insights. Share mini case studies, challenge assumptions, add tactical tips. Block 1-2 hours daily for this.
