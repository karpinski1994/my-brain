# LinkedIn Lead Generation

## Summary

LinkedIn lead generation works through lead magnets, strategic DM outreach, social selling in comments, and simple offer creation. The platform rewards value-first approaches over cold pitching.

## Lead Magnets

### 3-Step Framework
1. **Identify one burning problem** your prospect has
2. **Build a lead magnet** that solves that specific problem (Notion page, PDF, website)
3. **Market with psychology** — scarcity, anchoring, halo effect

### Lead Magnet Marketing Biases
- **Scarcity bias** — limited time availability
- **Anchoring bias** — state what people normally pay for this info, then give it free
- **Halo effect** — show how it worked for you/others, so it can work for them too

### Content Distribution for Lead Magnets
- Post providing insight into what they'll receive
- Give incomplete information so people want the full thing
- Add a visual element (screenshot, mockup) to increase perceived value
- Make free stuff as good as people's paid stuff

## DM Strategy (LinkedIn Outreach)

### The Right Approach
**Never** pitch slap. Generic "let's exchange synergies" is your competition — the bar is low.

1. Find mutual ground (shared event, interest, connection)
2. Start with a non-intrusive, slightly funny question
3. Build casual conversation first
4. Earn the right to ask

Example: "Hey [Name], I'm exhausted from [Event] — I see you were there too. Did you manage to grab the free snacks?"

Lead with fun, end with value. Never lead with a transaction.

### Finding Leads in Comments
Every time you post, scan your comment section for your ICP. If they're commenting, they're interested. Send a personalized connection request:

Script: "Hey [Name], thank you for commenting on my post — that was very kind. How's [topic] going for you this year?"

## Social Selling with Comments

### Pin Comment Strategy
Instead of putting a CTA in your post body (which reduces engagement), leave the CTA as a pinned comment. This:
- Keeps the post clean and value-focused
- Puts the CTA in front of already-interested readers
- The author's comment is always shown at the top
- Generated $10K+ from a single playbook without ever posting about it

### Sniper Method
1. Make a list of ideal clients/people you want to be seen by
2. Comment on their content when they post (be early)
3. Comments must be insanely valuable — they start sub-conversations with the author AND their audience
4. Builds trust with your ideal target through proximity

## Offer Creation

### Keep It Simple
- One problem → one solution → one offer
- Example: "5 LinkedIn posts/week, 1 call/month, weekly reviews"
- Remove friction: don't offer graphics, scheduling, and management alongside writing
- Focus on your strengths only

### Pricing Framework
- 10 clients at $1K each = $10K/month
- 5 clients at $2K = $10K/month
- 3 clients at $3.3K = $10K/month
- Raise price by $300-500 after closing each new deal

## The Flywheel

Your daily LinkedIn workflow should include: **posting, pitching, networking**. Each feeds the other:
- Content grows audience
- Networking builds relationships
- Pitching converts warm leads
