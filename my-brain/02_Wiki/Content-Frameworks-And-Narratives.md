# Content Frameworks & Narratives

## Summary

Copywriting frameworks and narrative structures for LinkedIn and Facebook content targeting senior devs. Each framework serves a different goal — use BAB for transformation stories, PAS for pain-point posts, AIDA for cold traffic, and StoryBrand for community building.

## Copywriting Frameworks (For Short-Form Posts)

### BAB (Before-After-Bridge) — Best for transformation stories
- **Before:** Describe a current, relatable problem (e.g., senior dev stuck earning local rates)
- **After:** Paint the desirable future (landing a US remote role, earning $8k+/month)
- **Bridge:** Your mentoring/service as the path between them
- **ICP example:** "Before: Earning €3k/month through an agency taking 60%. After: $8k/month direct with a US startup. Bridge: That's exactly what my 90-day program does."

### PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve) — Best for pain-point posts
- **Problem:** Name the specific struggle (e.g., "Senior Developer Plateau")
- **Agitate:** Twist the knife — every year waiting = $50k+ opportunity cost
- **Solve:** Present your solution as relief
- **ICP example:** "Problem: You're a senior dev who can't break into the US market. Agitate: Every month you wait, you're losing $5k+ to middlemen. Solve: Here's exactly how we bypass them in 90 days."

### AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action) — Best for cold traffic
- **Attention:** Bold hook within 2 seconds
- **Interest:** Fresh information related to their desire
- **Desire:** Social proof + benefits
- **Action:** One clear CTA
- **ICP example:** "Attention: Your US client is paying $120k for your work. Interest: You're seeing $35k. Here's why. Desire: Miguel went from $2k to $8.5k/month using this system. Action: DM me 'US' for the breakdown."

### The 4 U's — Best for short, punchy updates
- **Useful:** Does it provide actual value?
- **Urgent:** Reason to act now?
- **Unique:** Different from everything else in their feed?
- **Ultra-specific:** Focused on one point?
- **ICP example:** "The 3 negotiation tactics (Useful) that helped a Senior Java Dev increase his offer by 40% (Ultra-specific). My July cohort closes Friday (Urgent)."

### StoryBrand — Best for Facebook groups / community building
- **Character:** Your reader + their primary goal
- **Problem:** Internal or external struggle
- **Guide:** You, the mentor/expert
- **Plan:** Simple 3-step process
- **Success/Failure:** Victory vs. what's at stake
- **ICP example:** "You're a senior dev who wants international work (Character). The problem isn't your skills — it's that you're invisible to the right companies (Problem). I help devs like you get seen by US founders (Guide). Here's the 3-step system (Plan)."

### PASTOR — Most comprehensive (for high-ticket)
- **Person/Problem**
- **Amplify** the consequences
- **Story** / Testimonials
- **Transformation**
- **Offer**
- **Response**

### Star-Story-Solution — Best for case studies
- **Star:** A specific programmer (e.g., "Meet Alex")
- **Story:** The struggle (e.g., trying to enter the US market from abroad)
- **Solution:** How they used your service to land a $150k role

## Narrative Structures (For Long-Form Posts)

### Hero's Journey (The Quest)
Best for "founder stories" or deep-dive student transformations:
1. **Ordinary World** — Working for a local agency
2. **Call to Adventure** — Seeing a peer move into an international role
3. **Crossing the Threshold** — Joining your program
4. **The Reward** — New career path + higher income

### Fichtean Curve
Best for stories about the chaos of starting a business. Skip intros, drop reader into crisis:
- "It was 2 AM, the deployment failed, and I realized I didn't know how to run a business."
- Build through mini-crises until the climax (successful launch)

### Kishōtenketsu (East Asian 4-Act)
No conflict needed. Works via discovery + perspective shift:
1. **Introduction** — The current situation
2. **Development** — More detail
3. **Twist** — A surprising reveal
4. **Conclusion** — New understanding

### SCQA (Situation-Complication-Question-Answer)
Best for business analysis / thought leadership posts:
- **Situation:** "Senior devs in Latin America earn 3-5x less than US peers"
- **Complication:** "But the middleman takes 60% — not skill gap"
- **Question:** "What if you could bypass them entirely?"
- **Answer:** "Here's the exact framework our clients use."

### Pixar's Story Spine
Fill-in-the-blank template for quick storyboarding:
- "Once upon a time there was [developer]. Every day [they struggled]. One day [they discovered something]. Because of that [they changed]. Until finally [they achieved the goal]."

## LinkedIn-Specific Tips

- **Master the hook:** You have 2 lines before truncation. Make them count.
- **Focus on logic + ROI:** Senior professionals want facts. Use real numbers.
- **Address objections directly:** "I don't have time" → reframe as investment vs. cost of inaction.
- **Active tone:** Start with "Imagine," "Build," "Negotiate."
- **Clear CTA:** One low-friction action — "DM me 'Mentoring' for details."
- **Post structure:** Hook → Body (framework) → CTA.

## Framework Selection Guide

| Goal | Framework | Best For |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Transformation story | BAB | LinkedIn posts, ads |
| Pain-point engagement | PAS | Problem-aware audience |
| Cold traffic | AIDA | New followers, ads |
| Short urgent update | 4 U's | Community posts |
| Trust/community | StoryBrand | Facebook groups |
| Case study | Star-Story-Solution | Social proof |
| Thought leadership | SCQA | LinkedIn articles |
| Long-form story | Hero's Journey | "About me" posts |
| Quick template | Pixar Spine | Any narrative |
