## Self-Talk & Inner Critic

### Typical Inner Critic Script

> "Why the fuck this stupid shit is happening to me again?"
> "Why the fuck it has to always happen to ME?"
> "You are so stupid. Why the fuck did you do it? How the fuck you could be so idiot?"
> "What the fuck is wrong with this piece of shit?"

Pattern: **Outward blame** (at tools, creators, systems) but underneath it's a **perfectionist tantrum** — "this shouldn't be happening, and I shouldn't have to deal with it."

### Triggers for Inner Critic

- Stupid mistakes with big (or emotionally big) consequences
- Things not working as expected
- Tech failures (Premiere Pro crashes, losing work)
- When someone close thinks differently than expected

### Core Fears (When Going After What I Want)

1. **Failure** = having to work for someone stupider who just had the balls to act
2. **Expressing real opinions** → getting blacklisted, no one will employ me
3. **Being exposed** as over-promising / not delivering
4. **Wasting time** — everything being pointless
5. **Having to go back to Poland for a "normal job"** — the worst outcome

### Self-Protection Mechanisms (Walls & Avoidance)

1. **Over-preparation** — scripts, scenarios, animations, social proof, fact-checking — everything crafted so nothing can be criticized
2. **Safety net prep** — 1/3 of time goes to learning interview algorithms, preparing for failure instead of growing the business
3. **Escapism** — video games, sweets, YouTube, Twitch — detaching from reality
4. **Isolation** — avoiding people, not talking, hiding
5. **Perfectionist polishing** — keeps improving things that are already good enough instead of shipping

## Self-Sabotage

### Self-Sabotage Patterns

1. **Anger → quitting pipeline:** get angry → "I've had enough" → video games / escape
2. **Failure prep as avoidance:** spending time on interview prep and product improvement instead of promoting / sales / outreach
3. **The one-video spiral:** innocent distraction → hours lost → momentum killed
4. **Tech failure triggers:** Premiere bugs, lost work → rage quit
5. **Evening shutdown:** too tired/irritable to do anything useful — postpones everything to morning
6. **Food coma:** after first meal, afternoon productivity collapses

### What Kills Momentum

- Getting back to "stupid things" (League videos, YouTube) — "one innocent video" escalates
- A bad start to the day = the whole day is lost
- Once momentum is broken, it's very hard to rebuild same-day

### Avoidance Justifications

| Task | Internal Dialogue |
|------|-------------------|
| Posting content | "This still needs improvement" → endless polishing |
| Sales outreach (looms) | "So fucking boring, brainless, takes so much time" |
| Exercise | Not organized enough — sweat/tired messes up evening calls |
| Editing videos | "I fucking hate it" — even 100% success guarantee wouldn't make him do it personally |
| Taxes / forms | "Stupid corrupted government telling me what to do" |
| General boring tasks | Hardest when repetitive, not mentally challenging, dull |

### Core Lesson from Q24

> **Editing videos is not a discipline problem. It is a delegation problem.**
> Even with a 100% guarantee of success, the user would not edit videos himself. This task must be delegated or eliminated from his direct responsibility.

### Irrational Fear (Known to Be Irrational but Still Feels True)

> "If I say controversial things (criticizing outsourcing companies, corruption), I will be blacklisted from software engineering and no one will employ me."
>
> **Rational assessment:** Unlikely — I have many skills, could work outside software engineering, and most people won't care.
