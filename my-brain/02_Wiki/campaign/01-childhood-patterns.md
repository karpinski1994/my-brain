# Childhood & Origin Patterns

## Overview

A deep exploration of childhood — family dynamics, early messages, survival mechanisms, and the core beliefs that still influence today. The first step in understanding what patterns were programmed before I had a choice.

---

## Family of Origin

### General Feel

Childhood split into two distinct phases:

- **0–6 years:** Pure adventure. Forest exploration with dad, aunt, and mom. Building structures, playing soldier, survival games. Felt free, warm, super chill. Had a guinea pig — one of my best friends, very important to me. Spending time with cousins building fictional companies, having adventures.
- **6+ years:** School started, things got more complicated. Began sports acrobatics at 7–8. More emotions, more complexity. The accident at 6–7 where my forehead had to be sewn up. The house I built with my dad was destroyed by someone — a significant upset.

### Roles in the Family

- **Rebel** — pushed boundaries, confrontational
- **Achiever** — sports brought intense validation
- Not caretaker, peacemaker, invisible, or clown
- Described as "spoiled" and a "lonely child"

### Patterns Modeled by Parents

**Dad (entrepreneur):**
- Super smart, knows everything about everything (history, physics, chemistry, literature)
- Always curious — started learning English later in life and excelled
- Entrepreneurial, built his own company
- Loves sports — kayaking, skiing, very active
- Excellent public speaker — my friends enjoyed listening to him
- Sometimes prioritized workers above me
- His businesses collapsed 2–3 times — not from lack of skill, but from corruption and local authorities with connections. Witnessed this like a sponge.

**Mom:**
- "Childish" — gives an impression of being less capable than she actually is
- Stayed at home, sleeping when I left for school
- Didn't take care of herself physically
- Overprotective — embarrassed me at the swimming pool in front of friends, angry about the quarry trip with dad
- Easier to talk to than dad when I had problems

**Family Dynamics:**
- Shouting is how we express emotions — anger, passion, during Christmas fights about politics (typical Polish family)
- Emotional expression through volume, not suppression

### Moments of Feeling Unsafe, Unseen, or Not Enough

- Mom's overprotection: took me away from the diving tower at the pool in front of friends, flipped out about the quarry
- At school: mocked by brother of a friend after I beat him (he was mocking me first)
- The bicycle fight: I knocked a kid off his bike to take it, he came back with his dad who grabbed me by the neck — I panicked
- After those fights, became scared of starting fights for a while. Became more reserved.

## Early Messages & Beliefs

### Direct Messages from Environment

- "Talented, intelligent, you'll take care of yourself" — from peers and community
- Pressure to continue sports acrobatics or take over dad's company — regardless of what I wanted

### Absorbed Beliefs

**From the trainer (Soviet-era coaching style):**
- Started with a perfect score, every mistake deducted
- Constant pointing out of errors, almost no praise
- Harsh, old-school approach, lots of shouting
- **Result:** Until today, I am focused on mistakes. Undervalue myself and my work. Transmit this pattern to people I care about (but not to workers/students — I protect them from it).

**From the community (peers at the gym):**
- Creating videos about sports acrobatics — mocked by gym friends ("stupid music," etc.)
- Outside the bubble: everyone said they were amazing
- **Result:** Standing out and exposing yourself outside the box gets you punished. Better to hide.

**From the broader community (town):**
- Outside the gym: local celebrity from TV, girls signing up to meet me, things easier everywhere
- Inside the gym: outcast, couldn't relate, not part of the group
- **Result:** Two alternative identities. Celebrated on the outside, outcast on the inside.

**From dad's business experience:**
- "Who goes to work doesn't have time for business."
- Businesses collapsed from corruption, not lack of skill
- **Result:** "Doing business is dangerous. Better to stay hidden. They will get you." The 90s Polish entrepreneurial trauma absorbed second-hand.

**Money:**
- Money was never a problem in the family
- Used to feel awkward taking money for work — wanted to do things for free
- Overcame this by starting my own business
- As an employee, always asked for raises confidently (knew my value)
- Parental safety net: they're in a good financial situation, can always help. This is a double-edged sword — comfort but also prevents installing full self-sufficiency.
- Despite some mistakes, always had savings, never had debts.

### Work & Success

- Achievement was appreciated and attractive — local celebrity, dopamine from shows/competitions
- Felt unstoppable, that I could do more
- Things were easier everywhere because of recognition
- Protected by older friends — brotherhood-like atmosphere
- **But:** The inner bubble (gym community) punished the same achievements that the outer world celebrated

## Survival Mechanisms (Coping Strategies Developed in Childhood)

### What I Did

1. **Kept problems to myself until the last moment** — never told parents about gym struggles until breaking point
2. **Used criticism as motivation** — "I'll show you" mindset when peers tried to bring me down
3. **Pretended things didn't bother me** — external mask of unbothered confidence
4. **Fought back / confrontational** — made enemies because I never backed down from mockery
5. **Distracted myself** — video games, especially when overwhelmed
6. **Hid myself** — avoided people, didn't talk, isolated
7. **Sought safe spaces** — forest, certain friends, some activities

### What I Still Use Today

| Strategy | Still Active? | Does It Serve Me? |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Keeping problems to myself | No longer | — |
| Criticism as motivation | Yes | Mixed — drives but burns |
| Pretending it doesn't bother me | Yes | Keeps mask on, prevents vulnerability |
| Confrontational | Yes — Achilles heel | No — treat loved ones as enemies over disagreements |
| Distraction (video games) | Yes | Escape mechanism, unclear if serving |
| Hiding / avoiding people | Yes | Prevents connection |

| Fear of exposing myself / emotions | Community punished standing out; hiding became safe |
| Keeping real problems to myself | Childhood strategy of handling it alone |
| Hiding behind a mask | "Pretend it doesn't bother me" strategy |
