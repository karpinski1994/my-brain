# LinkedIn Profile Optimization

## Summary

Your LinkedIn profile acts as a money-making landing page. Every element — banner, headline, featured section, profile photo — must be optimized to convert visitors into leads.

## Banner

- Size: 2400x600px
- Must communicate what you do and who you help
- Include a clear call to action (where to buy, contact, or sign up)
- Design should be simple, clear, straight to the point
- Use social proof logos (Forbes, clients, media features) — each logo targets a different demographic
- At the start, a solid brand color works; upgrade as you grow

## Featured Section

The most underutilized real estate on LinkedIn. Most people pin a viral post — this wastes prime conversion space.

### 3-Tier Structure
- **Low ticket** — newsletter sign-up, free template, PDF ($0-$100)
- **Mid ticket** — 1:1 consulting, single session
- **High ticket** — retainer application, premium offer ($1000+)

Each tier needs a custom thumbnail (treat like a YouTube thumbnail: clear, enticing, curiosity-driven). Use the + button to add links with thumbnails.

## Headline

The headline + profile photo are the only elements consistently shown in the feed. Must answer: "What promise am I making to my ideal visitor?"

### Formula (with offer)
`What you do + Who you do it for + How you help`
Example: "I help business coaches get more sales through DMs"

### Formula (without offer)
`On a mission to [cool thing] with [cool outcome] — sharing content about [topic]`
Example: "On a mission to help entrepreneurs grow — sharing content about marketing and AI"

### Advanced Formula (2026+)
`[Number] [clients] past [result] with [method] — free [lead magnet] below`
Example: "Scaled 47 e-commerce brands past $5M with email automation — free case study below"

## Profile Photo

- High quality, well-lit, clear background
- You must look like the expert you want to be perceived as
- No holiday photos, outdated selfies, or group shots
- iPhone with white background works; Canva backgrounds help
- Color background (green, purple, blue) reads cleaner
- "People buy from what they like" — look like your ideal client's trusted advisor

## Social Proof Stacking

Social proof should appear everywhere:
- **Headline** — "like Kim Kardashian did" (halo effect example)
- **Banner** — logos of companies/publications
- **Featured section** — testimonials, case studies
- **Content** — client results, screenshots, before/after

If you have no social proof: work with people for free, get testimonials, use them until you earn the logos.
