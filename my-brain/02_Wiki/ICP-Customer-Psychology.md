# ICP Customer Psychology

## Summary

The "Rockstar Programmer" ICP operates in a duality: internally they blame themselves (imposter syndrome, skill doubts), externally they blame the system (middlemen, recruiters, local markets). Effective content speaks to both layers simultaneously.

## Internal Blocks (Self-Blame)

### 1. Imposter Syndrome / Skill Doubts
- "I feel like maybe I'm not good enough for this kind of opportunity"
- "I don't know if it's realistic for me to be that ambitious"
- "Maybe I'm undershooting myself regarding my knowledge"
- "I definitely feel too small in my shoes for a senior position yet"
- **Key insight:** Even 15-year veterans feel this. They compare themselves to FAANG engineers.

### 2. Confusion & Not Knowing
- "I don't even know what the problem anymore"
- "I don't know how to find these opportunities outside my country"
- "I don't have a proper roadmap on how to start my career"

### 3. Self-Identified Blockers
- "My CV wasn't good enough"
- "I haven't tried searching on my own"
- "I don't have a university degree — that seems quite problematic"
- "I lost my ability to hold a conversation in English"

### 4. Self-Sabotage Patterns
- Passive approach: "I'm not applying, they are reaching out to me"
- Procrastination: "I just started this year to seriously look"
- Inaction: "I haven't tried searching on my own"
- Blaming circumstances: "It will always depend on my mood"

### 5. Hopelessness
- "I have neither hope nor hope that they choose me"
- "I just gave up"
- "The motivation is decreased, patience as well"

## External Obstacles (System Blame)

### 1. Companies & Middlemen (26/32 calls)
- **The primary villain.** Exploitative outsourcing, bad management, unwilling to pay.
- "Middleman companies which try to get a piece of your earnings"
- "The intermediary was there just to pass money from one hand to another"
- "Raising my hourly by $1 is a problem because they don't want to earn less"

### 2. Local Market Limitations (16/32 calls)
- "Geography is destiny" — trapped by low local salaries.
- "In Chile, locally it's very complicated" / "In Cuba they pay very little"
- No local tech scene, economic inflation eating their salary.

### 3. HR & Recruiters (14/32 calls)
- Gatekeepers who don't understand tech.
- Ghosting, keyword matching, arbitrary rejection.
- "It was never about my actual skills, it was about what I want to be paid"

### 4. The System Itself (11/32 calls)
- "Playing the interview game" vs. real work.
- Leetcode irrelevant to actual job.
- Double interview process with middlemen.

### 5. Saturation & Competition (9/32 calls)
- "100+ applicants on every LinkedIn job"
- "Thousands of applicants" feeling.

### 6. AI Threat (2/32 calls)
- Emerging fear: AI replacing junior roles.
- Not yet mainstream but growing.

## Emotional Drivers ("The Why Now")

### 1. Financial Fear / Survival Mode
- "If I lose this job, I don't know what I'm going to do"
- "I don't have any savings"
- "I just live paycheck to paycheck"

### 2. Family & Future Security
- "After 16 meetings, I just want to go to sleep" — can't be present for family
- "I want to pass more time with my son"
- The promise to a father / desire for generational change

### 3. Burnout & Mental Health
- "I start to get depressed when I see myself doing the same thing"
- "It bothers me to wake up and see requests from colleagues first thing"
- "I was off for 4 or 5 months — just quit one morning"

### 4. Professional Pride
- "I'm the first person in my bank's history who made an internal application"
- "I know my work is worth more than this"
- "I want to create a name for myself, not depend on a CV"

## The Psychological Duality

Effective content must acknowledge **both** sides simultaneously:
- "The system is rigged against you (external) AND you've been playing it wrong (internal)"
- "You have 15 years of experience (reality) AND you feel like a junior (perception)"
- "Your skills are world-class (truth) AND your strategy is local (gap)"

This duality creates the tension that drives action — the external validation ("it's not your fault") combined with personal accountability ("but only you can fix it").
