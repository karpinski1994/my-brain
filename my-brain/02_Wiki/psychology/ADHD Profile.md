# ADHD Profile

## Summary

ASRS-v1.1 screening score: **3/6** (threshold is 4). While below clinical cutoff, the profile shows significant executive function challenges: procrastination, difficulty completing tasks, poor time planning, emotional dysregulation, and chronic sleep disruption.

## Key Ideas

- **Part A score**: 3/6 — symptoms highly suggestive but subclinical
- **Core symptoms**: lack of attention to detail, disorganization, misplacing possessions, procrastination, task completion difficulty, low motivation, time blindness
- **Sleep**: chronic night waking (family pattern), waking exhausted even after 14h sleep
- **Emotional**: irritability, low frustration tolerance, emotional dysregulation
- **Work pattern**: initial excitement → burnout cycle, thrives on inspiration not deadlines
- **Gaming**: 10-12h/day LoL consumption — dopamine-seeking avoidance behavior

## Interpretation

The ADHD tendencies interact strongly with the [[Core Wounds]] and [[MBTI Evolution]]. The procrastination → guilt → more procrastination loop is amplified by perfectionism and fear of judgment. The low score may reflect coping mechanisms developed through years of discipline (sports, programming).
