# MBTI Evolution

## Summary

Four distinct personality types measured over six years, reflecting deep adaptation to life circumstances rather than a fixed identity. The progression shows a shift from extraverted feeling to introverted thinking, with increasing analytical dominance.

## Key Ideas

| Year | Type | Role | Strategy | Key Trait |
|------|------|------|----------|-----------|
| 2019 | ENFP-T | Diplomat | Social Engagement | Extraverted 85%, Feeling 54% |
| 2021 | ENTJ-A | Analyst | People Mastery | Extraverted 74%, Thinking 60% |
| 2022 | ISTJ-A | Sentinel | Confident Individualism | Introverted 58%, Thinking 80% |
| 2025 | INTP-T | Analyst | Constant Improvement | Introverted 54%, Thinking 75% |

- **ENFP (2019)**: Creative, enthusiastic, emotional — base nature
- **ENTJ (2021)**: Action mode — purposefulness, management, leadership
- **ISTJ (2022)**: Forced adaptation to programmer/entrepreneur role — discipline, reliability
- **INTP (2025)**: Return to reflection — intellectual, analytical, explorer

## Interpretation

The evolution shows an "actor of many roles" — capable of shifting style to meet environmental demands. The Thinking trait has become dominant (60% → 80% → 75%), while Extraversion flipped to Introversion. This suggests growing analytical dominance and declining social energy needs.
