# Relationship Patterns

## Summary

The relationship with Regina — "the love of my life" — is undermined by conflict patterns that mirror childhood wounds. Small triggers (e.g., unwashed dishes) escalate into full-blown fights with name-calling, blame, and past-referencing.

## Key Ideas

- **Trigger → Explosion cycle**: minor irritation (unwashed dishes) → harsh criticism → defensiveness → snowball fight
- **Projection**: Regina triggers the same feelings of neglect as mother (who slept late, didn't pack lunch)
- **My behavior**: name-calling, past mistakes weaponized, distrust accusations — "worst version of myself"
- **Mutual patterns**: both repeat toxic schemas from family of origin
- **Regina's stance**: aware of patterns, open to working on communication, pushing for professional help
- **Goal**: stop being a "slave to anger and irritation over insignificant things"
