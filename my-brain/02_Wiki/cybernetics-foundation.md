# Cybernetics Foundation

The science of steering — a meta-language for understanding purposeful interaction in any system, from individuals to societies.

## Summary

Cybernetics (kybernetes = helmsman) is the science of steering: purposeful interaction toward a future goal. Unlike physics which analyzes past causes, cybernetics envisions a future state and determines present actions. The Polish Cybernetics School (Marian Mazur, Józef Kossecki) developed a rigorous framework that was suppressed under communism, then validated internationally (NASA hired Mazur).

## Key Concepts

### Definition
- **Steering** = purposeful interaction (acting on a system with a specific future goal)
- **Physics vs. Cybernetics**: Past causes vs. future goals
- Cybernetics is a **meta-language** — a unified semantic framework to prevent miscommunication across disciplines

### History
- Ancient Greece: Steering ships and states
- Roman Empire: Sidewalk curbs for soldiers (conserving mental energy)
- WWI: Anti-aircraft needed future trajectory prediction → cybernetics re-emerged
- Polish School: Marian Mazur (destroyed in 1944 Warsaw Uprising, banned under communism)
- Józef Kossecki: Sociocybernetics, accurately predicted modern geopolitics before 2015

### Three Domains of Cognition (Mazur)
1. **Art**: Assumptions + Impressions (emotional, no proof needed)
2. **Philosophy**: Assumptions + Theorems (explanations, no proof procedure)
3. **Science**: Assumptions + Theorems + Proof Procedures (falsifiable, repeatable)

### AI & Consciousness
- AI = "Information Algorithms" (not actual intelligence)
- AI has consciousness (pathways of correlative conductivity) but NOT free will (no Homeostat)
- AI lacks a Homeostat → cannot generate its own goals → just a tool
- Human brain: 10,000 connections × 80 billion neurons, running on 20 watts

## Key Insight

The greatest friction in modern organizations is lack of unified semantic code — people using different definitions for the same words. Cybernetics provides a strict structural language to eliminate this.
