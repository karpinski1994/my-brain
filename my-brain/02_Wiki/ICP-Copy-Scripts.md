# ICP Copy Scripts

## Summary

Two copy components for content and ads: (1) emotional pain point paragraphs that agitate the ICP's deepest frustrations, and (2) "Mini History" first-person stories that build rapport through shared experience. Use these as swipe files for LinkedIn posts, Facebook ads, and email sequences.

## Component 1: Pain Point Paragraphs

Use these to open content, ads, or videos. They agitate the specific pain BEFORE offering the solution.

### The Application Black Hole
"…because after the 47th application, you stop refreshing email with hope. You already know what's there. Nothing. You tweak the CV. Again. Rewrite the cover letter. Again. Another 'We decided to move forward with other candidates.' Or worse — silence. You're not junior. You've shipped real things. But somehow you're competing with 900 people for one role, and it feels random. Like a lottery you didn't ask to enter."

### The Location Tax
"…you're doing the same work as engineers abroad. Same tickets. Same pressure. Same late calls. But somehow your paycheck tells a different story. They call it 'market rates.' You call it location tax. Or a middleman skimming off the top while you do the heavy lifting."

### The Broken Learning Loop
"…another platform. Another promise. 'This one works if you just stay consistent.' So you spend nights polishing a portfolio no one opens. Sending proposals that disappear. Watching months of effort turn into zero replies. At some point you stop telling friends you're 'in the process.' Because it sounds fake. Even to you."

### The Boredom / Stagnation
"…you pretend you're fine at work. You deliver. You smile. You close tickets. But inside, you're bored and quietly angry. You're treated like a keyboard. Not an engineer. Not a thinker. Just 'implement this' and don't ask too many questions. The fear isn't burning out. It's waking up a year from now in the exact same place."

### The Comparison Pain
"You scroll LinkedIn at 11 p.m. and see another guy from your batch posting 'Just landed $180k remote.' Same stack. Same years. He's in Colombia too. You close the app. You've sent 347 applications this year. Three replies. Zero offers."

### The Seniority Paradox
"You're senior. You know it. Your code reviews are clean, your PRs get approved in hours, clients love you. Yet every single job post says '5+ years' and still ghosts you after the first round. You watch juniors from the US get callbacks while you rewrite the same 'tell me about yourself' for the 47th time."

### The Survival Fear
"Payday comes and you already know the number before you open the app. Not enough for the loan. Not enough for your mom's medicine. Definitely not enough to breathe. You close the banking app, open LinkedIn, see the same 'We're hiring remotely' posts, and feel that familiar punch in the gut."

## Component 2: Mini History Scripts

First-person stories using the "I was there too…" format. Builds instant rapport by showing shared experience, then pivots to the solution. 50-75 words each.

### The Middleman Escape (Strongest)
"I was there too… working on serious projects, but always through middlemen. Clients paid premium US rates, and I got the leftovers. I wasn't treated like a partner — just a pair of hands. I thought maybe I needed more years, more certifications, more luck. But the truth hurt more: I had the skills, just not the positioning. Once I fixed that, everything changed fast."

### The Ghosting Story
"I was there too… sending hundreds of resumes into a black hole. Out of 500, maybe one reply. Ghosting everywhere. I felt stuck, underpaid, watching others earn 5x for the same code just because of location. Honestly, I was scared — no savings, paycheck to paycheck. Then I realized the problem wasn't my skills. I stopped playing their game… and learned a completely different one."

### The Full Journey (Patryk's Story)
"I started in a Polish startup at $500 a month. Boss promised a bright future but kept 60%. Same exact code as devs from UK, USA and Finland making $110k a year — I got peanuts just because of my passport. Sent hundreds of CVs to the UK — total silence. Then I stopped applying like everyone else. Completely changed the game. Landed my first job with a Scottish startup… from Poland. Jumped from $1,500 to over $5,000 a month. Five years later — $17,000."

### The Imposter Syndrome Angle
"I was there too… telling myself I wasn't good enough. That maybe the problem was my skills. My English. My experience. I kept blaming myself. Meanwhile my boss was pocketing 60% of what clients paid for my code. Developers from the UK doing identical work were earning $110k a year. I was earning a fraction. Not because I was worse. Because I was born in the wrong place."

### The Family Pressure Angle
"I was there too… living paycheck to paycheck. No savings. If I lost my job tomorrow — I didn't know what I'd do. And I had people depending on me. I was writing code in Poland for $500 a month. My boss kept 60% of what clients paid for my work. I wanted $6k a month. Remote work. Stability. Freedom. So I sent hundreds of CVs. Ghosting. Black hole. Nothing. Then I stopped and asked myself — what if the whole approach is wrong?"

### The Common Enemy Angle (Strongest for Ads)
"I was there too… working for a company that was quietly taking 60% of everything clients paid for my work. I didn't even know at first. I found out when I worked alongside developers from the UK and USA — same code, same pull requests — earning five times more than me. Not because they were better. Because there was no middleman between them and the client. That was the moment I understood. The enemy isn't your skills. It's not your English. It's a system designed to keep developers from poorer countries cheap and replaceable."

### The No Strategy Angle
"I was there too… with no strategy. Honestly, just throwing CVs into the void and hoping. Ghosting. Black hole. Zero replies. And deep down thinking — maybe I'm not good enough. I was in a Polish startup earning $500 a month while my boss pocketed 60% of my work's value. I didn't want much. Just $5k a month. Remote. No middlemen. Freedom to breathe. But I had no roadmap. No strategy. No idea what I was doing wrong. Until the day I stopped applying… and built something they couldn't ignore."

### The Legacy Code Frustration
"I was there too… doing the same thing every day. Maintaining legacy code. No growth. Waking up dreading the work. I started to feel like I was disappearing. $500 a month in a Polish startup. Boss keeping 60%. And right next to me — developers from the UK doing exciting, cutting-edge work — making $110k a year. I wanted to work on real products. Modern stacks. International teams. $6k, $7k a month. So I started applying like crazy. Hundreds of CVs. Pure silence. Then I realized — the problem wasn't my skills. I was just invisible to the right companies."

## Production Notes

- **Format:** Each script is 20-40 seconds when spoken naturally
- **Voice:** First-person Patryk, raw and conversational, not polished marketing
- **Structure:** Pain → Realization → Pivot → Tease ("That method is called…")
- **Key rule:** Always end with a curiosity gap that leads to the offer
