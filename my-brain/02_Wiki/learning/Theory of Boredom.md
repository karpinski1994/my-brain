# Theory of Boredom

> From the Octalysis Framework and Flow Theory.

## Core Concept

Boredom occurs when **challenge is too low** relative to a person's **skill level** (Flow Theory). In gamification, the Octalysis Framework addresses boredom through specific intrinsic and Black-Hat drives.

## Key Drives to Combat Boredom

### 1. Core Drive 7: Unpredictability & Curiosity
- **Primary drive** for combating boredom
- Leverages the suspense of not knowing what will happen next
- Uses **Variable Rewards** to create dopamine hits — more effective than fixed rewards for sustaining interest
- Examples: mystery chests, randomized bonuses, surprise rewards

### 2. Core Drive 3: Empowerment of Creativity & Feedback
- "Evergreen Mechanic" — prevents tasks from feeling like static grind
- Skill Trees, Custom Loadouts, experimentation
- Used in the Scaffolding Phase to keep the core loop engaging

### 3. Core Drive 2: Development & Accomplishment
- Progress bars, leveling up, visible advancement
- Weighted XP for high-resistance tasks

## Applications

- Make dull content exciting with surprise rewards
- Allow autonomous expression and experimentation
- Use narrative wrappers to reframe boring tasks
- Speedrunning for flow (artificially increase challenge)
