# Hook Writing Framework

## Summary

A hook's only job: stop the scroll and make the viewer opt in. It needs Topic Clarity (crystal clear what the content is about) and On-Target Curiosity (viewer believes it's for them).

## The 4 Mistakes (And Their Solutions)

### 1. Delay → Speed to Value
Introduce the topic within 1-2 seconds. Every second of delay sheds viewers.
- Short, punchy, staccato sentences
- Maximize density of value per word
- No warm-up, no throat-clearing

### 2. Confusion → Maximize Clarity
- Fewer words, simpler words (6th grade reading level)
- Direct active voice, not passive
- Cannot be misunderstood or interpreted multiple ways

### 3. Irrelevance → Establish Relevance
- Frame around "you" and "your," not "me" or "I"
- Agitate a known pain point the viewer already has
- Solve a "need to have," not a "nice to have"

### 4. Disinterest → Drive Curiosity (Contrast Formula)
The **3-step contrast formula:**

1. **Context Lean In** — Establish topic clarity by referencing a benefit or pain point
2. **Scroll Stop Interjection** — Use "but," "however," "yet" to stun
3. **Contrarian Snapback** — Go opposite direction of the lean, snapping viewer onto a new path

Example: "You've been writing LinkedIn posts for months with no results (Lean In)… but (Interjection) what if the problem isn't your content, it's your hook? (Snapback)"

### 5. Visuals
Visual hooks are 100x more powerful than spoken word alone.
- On-screen title text (3-5 words) reinforcing the lean
- Bold, big font
- Just enough motion to hold attention

## Proven Headline Patterns

From the greatest headlines in history — adapt for the rockstar programmer ICP:

### The How-To Pattern
- "How a complete beginner who [struggled]… accidentally discovered [result]"
- "How to [achieve goal] without [pain point]"
- "How spending $5 can save you $500"
- **ICP adaptation:** "How a senior dev from Brazil doubled his income by removing one thing: the middleman"

### The Curiosity Gap
- "What [group] won't tell you about [topic]"
- "The secret to [result] that nobody talks about"
- "Why some people [achieve result] while others [fail]"
- **ICP adaptation:** "What your agency won't tell you: your US client is paying 2x what you're getting"

### The Specific Promise
- "Give me [time] and I'll prove I can [result]"
- "Double [metric] in [timeframe] — 100% guaranteed"
- "[X] simple tactics to increase [desired outcome]"
- **ICP adaptation:** "Give me 90 days and I'll prove you can land a US remote role — or your money back"

### The Direct Address
- "To [specific person] who [specific pain]"
- "Are you making these mistakes when [activity]?"
- "Who else wants to [desire]?"
- **ICP adaptation:** "To the senior developer earning $4k/month while doing $120k worth of work"

### The Contrarian
- "Everything you know about [topic] is wrong"
- "The ugly truth about [pleasant-sounding thing]"
- "[Common belief] — but here's what actually works"
- **ICP adaptation:** "Your CV isn't the problem. Your strategy is. Here's why."

### The Specific Person
- "To [demographic] who [pain]"
- "Advice for [group] from someone who [credibility]"
- "If you [condition], you can [result]"
- **ICP adaptation:** "To the Latin American senior dev who's tired of being a 'good trade' for their company"

### The Bold Claim
- "The fastest way I know to [result]"
- "Discover how to [achieve result] without [pain]"
- "Finally… someone has discovered [solution]"
- **ICP adaptation:** "The fastest way I know to land a US remote role — no Leetcode, no cold applying"

### Time-Pressure / Specific Offer
- "Give me X days and I'll prove [result]"
- "X techniques to [outcome] — starting today"
- "How I [achieved result] in [short timeframe]"
- **ICP adaptation:** "7 days to a LinkedIn profile that gets US recruiters sliding into your DMs"

### Question Hook
- "Do you [specific pain]?"
- "What would you rather do — [painful thing] or [desired thing]?"
- "Can you spot the [mistake/opportunity]?"
- **ICP adaptation:** "What would you rather do — spend 4 hours on Leetcode or 4 hours with your family?"
