# 30-Day Content Strategy

## Summary

A 30-day LinkedIn content plan for the Rockstar Programmer business targeting CEE developers stuck in low-pay legacy code roles. Rotates 5 content pillars: Market Insights, Case Studies, Tips, Hot Takes, Success Stories.

## Core Philosophy

**Content is NOT educational — it's diagnostic.**

- ❌ "How to write a CV"
- ❌ "How to learn React"
- ✅ "Why your CV doesn't work"
- ✅ "Why you're underpaid"

## Content Mix

- **80% text, 20% video** to start (LinkedIn is a text platform; faster hook testing)
- Long-term: 4-5 text posts + 1-2 video posts per week
- **Hybrid strategy**: Test hooks as text first; if it works, record as video
- Posts are NOT about you — they're about what the **client thinks**

## 5 Content Pillars

1. **Market Insights** — reframe how the market works
2. **Case Studies** — real client stories with specific results
3. **Tips** — actionable advice (B2B vs employment, positioning)
4. **Hot Takes** — polarizing opinions that drive engagement
5. **Success Stories** — emotional transformation narratives

## 30-Day Schedule

### Week 1
1. Market Insight — "You are not stuck because the market is bad..."
2. Case Study (Victor) — 10y experience still underpaid → positioned for premium
3. Tip — "We don't sponsor visas" is a fake problem (B2B bypass)
4. Hot Take — LeetCode is a trap (optimizing exam perf, not market positioning)
5. Success Story — "Maybe I'm not good enough..." (confidence + positioning shift)
6. Market Insight — "The biggest lie: 'Just apply more'"
7. Case Study — Same skills, different game

### Week 2
1. Tip — Your CV is not the problem (entry point > presentation)
2. Market Insight — Competing with 1000 others in a volume game
3. Case Study — "I've tried everything" → removed dependency on job boards
4. Hot Take — Job boards are dead (rejection system, not hiring process)
5. Success Story — confidence + positioning problem, not skill issue
6. Market Insight — Real problem is not salary, it's the structure (middle layers)
7. Tip — Stop applying, start positioning

### Week 3 (More Aggressive)
1. Case Study — 10y experience treated like a junior (commodity trap)
2. Market Insight — Not underpaid, mispositioned (same skills, different segment)
3. Tip — Stop trying to be better; reposition instead
4. Hot Take — You're not stuck, you're comfortable
5. Success Story — "Senior operating like a junior" → clarity + strategy
6. Case Study — 2 years of applications, nothing → system problem
7. Market Insight — "Just keep applying" is the biggest scam

### Week 4 (Authority + Closing)
1. Tip — Not more time, better system
2. Case Study — Didn't want more money, wanted stability → predictable pipeline
3. Market Insight — Not more opportunities, better ones
4. Hot Take — Most developers don't actually want to earn more
5. Success Story — "I finally understood how the market works"
6. Market Insight — You're closer than you think (leverage, not skills)
7. Case Study — Thought needed another course → needed strategy + execution

## One Testimonial = 3-5 Posts

Example (Victor):
- Post 1: Middleman problem

- Post 3: Co-execution as differentiator

## Funnel / Distribution Strategy

### Phase 1 (Growth) — NOW
- 80% content on co-founder's profile (recruiter POV)
- Goal: lead volume
- Use "insider POV" — "I see this every day working with developers..."

### Phase 2 (Authority) — LATER
- Repost to your own profile
- Build personal brand

### Phase 3 (Scale)
- Both profiles active with different angles
- Her: market / hiring insights
- You: system / execution

### Growth Hack
Same post, 2 versions:
- Her profile: "I see this every day as a recruiter..."
- Your profile: "I've seen this working with 30+ developers..."
- Same content, 2 perspectives = 2x distribution

- "I've sent 500 CVs and nothing happened."
- "Maybe I'm just not good enough."
- "I feel stuck no matter what I try."
- "I'm tired of being underpaid."
- "I don't know what I'm doing wrong."
- "I feel like I'm competing with everyone."
- "I've done courses and nothing changed."
- "I'm working hard but going nowhere."
- "I don't want to be just another developer."

## Key Insights

- Posts about **client's thoughts**, not about you
- Case study formula: HOOK (client quote) → BEFORE (chaos) → FALSE BELIEF → SHIFT (your system) → AFTER (specifics)
- Every post should be diagnostic, not educational — reframe how they see their problem
- Numbers + specifics = 2x conversion
- Strong opinions = polarization = reach + inbound
