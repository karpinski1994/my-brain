# LinkedIn Content Strategy & Writing

## Summary

LinkedIn content strategy is built on content pillars, consistent posting with a clear educational/storytelling split, and writing that is optimized for mobile scanning. The algorithm now rewards expertise over volume.

## Content Pillars

Develop 3-5 content pillars based on your unfair advantages:
1. **Core expertise** — what you know best
2. **Market demand** — what people need right now
3. **Personal differentiators** — unique experiences, hobbies, perspectives

For each pillar, list 5-7 subtopics. This ensures you never run out of ideas.

### Rule of One (Topic)
The single biggest growth hack: **one problem, one solution, one thing**. Micro-niche down to where your expertise truly lies.
- Broad topics (entrepreneurship, business) compete with everyone
- Micro-niche (LinkedIn ghostwriting for B2B founders) — you're the only one

## Posting Frequency

- **Start**: 3-4 quality posts per week (enough to grow without burnout)
- **Advanced**: 5-7 posts per week
- **Quality over quantity**: one great post beats 10 mediocre ones
- Treat LinkedIn like a testing ground — every post gives data

## Content Mix

### The 80/20 Split
- **80% Educational** — tactical advice, how-to's, frameworks, case studies
- **20% Personal/Storytelling** — behind-the-scenes, lessons, transformation

### The 3 Content Goals
Every post must do at least one: **educate, inspire, entertain**. Doing two = winning. Doing all three = viral.

### Broad, Narrow, Niche
- **Broad** — appeals to mass audience, gets followers
- **Narrow** — builds relatability and relevance
- **Niche** — signals expertise, attracts high-intent leads

## Writing for LinkedIn

### The Hook
- 8 words max in sentence one
- Must contain 2+ keywords (novelty, curiosity, phomo)
- Clear outcome must be obvious
- Followed by a re-hook in sentence two (challenges the first statement)

### The Body
- Short sentences: 8-10 words max
- One line, space, next line (optimize for mobile scrolling)
- Bullet points over paragraphs
- Simple words, not complex (5th grade level)
- Describe problems better than your reader can
- Use numbers and specifics

### The CTA
Keep it clean. Don't crowd the post with a sales pitch. Better: drop the CTA in a **pinned comment**.

## Writing Frameworks

### SLay Framework (Storytelling)
**S**tory → **L**esson → **A**ction/Advice → **Y**ou
1. Open with a personal story
2. Extract the lesson
3. Give actionable advice
4. End with a "you" element (question, CTA, platitude)

### PAS Framework (Educational)
**P**roblem → **A**gitate → **S**olution
1. Identify the problem (hook)
2. Agitate why it hurts
3. Deliver step-by-step solution

## Visual Elements

- Every post should have a photo or image
- Carousels and infographics outperform text-only
- Photos should position you as an expert (speaking on stage, podcast, networking event)
- Visual rhythm: formatting matters more than most realize
- Images of the actual problem being solved (screenshots, calendars) increase perceived value

## Repetition Builds Authority

The #1 mistake LinkedIn creators make: not repeating themselves enough. Fear of sounding repetitive kills growth.

Baader-Meinhof frequency bias: the more you post about one thing, the more people associate you with it. Repetition doesn't bore people — it reinforces.

80% of content should be about your core topic. Signature style series make your content recognizable.
