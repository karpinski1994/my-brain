# Goal: Action Plan & Daily Systems

## What This Does

Takes steps 7-10 of the Brian Tracy method and pairs them with the worksheet's daily execution system. You'll list everything needed, organize it into a sequenced plan, identify your most important tasks, and set up daily and weekly rhythms.

## Questions

### Step 1: Brain Dump — Everything Needed

1. List everything you will have to do to achieve your goal. No judgment, no ordering — just capture every step, task, action, and milestone. Combine the obstacles, skills, and people from the previous step. Add as you think of more.

2. What are 10 core actions that, if taken, would move you significantly toward your goal?

3. Of those 10, which 3 are the most impactful? Which would create the most momentum?

### Step 2: Organize into a Plan

4. Organize your list by sequence: What do you have to do before something else? What's the logical order?

5. Organize by priority: What's most important? What's less important? The 80/20 Rule says 80% of results come from 20% of activities.

6. Group tasks into phases or categories. A "journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step." What's Phase 1?

### Step 3: Create the Timeline

7. Create a realistic timeline from today to your goal: Yearly → Monthly → Weekly breakdown.

8. Define what progress looks like this month. What needs to happen by the end of this month?

9. Define what needs to happen this week.

10. What's the #1 most important task for today?

### Step 4: Daily Execution Plan

11. What does your ideal daily execution rhythm look like? Consider your natural energy patterns (are you a morning person, night owl?).

12. What 3-6 key actions form your daily non-negotiable checklist?

13. What time blocks will you use? (e.g., 8-10am deep work, 10-11am admin, etc.)

14. What's your plan for "starting every day with your hardest/highest-leverage task"?

15. What tools will you use? (Habit tracker, calendar, to-do system, journal.)

### Step 5: Identify #1 Priority

16. Ask: "If I could only do one thing from this entire list, which one activity is most important right now?" Put a "#1" next to it.

17. Then: "If I could only do one other thing, what would it be?" And so on, until you have at least 7 top tasks organized by priority.

### Step 6: Weekly Review System

18. Every Sunday, you'll reflect on these four questions. Set them up now:
    - Did I follow my daily actions this week?
    - What slowed me down?
    - What was a win?
    - What will I change next week?

19. Where will you keep your weekly review? (Journal, Notion, Obsidian note, etc.)

20. Commit to your first week of execution. What will you do tomorrow to start?
