# Past: Childhood & Origin Patterns

## What This Does

Guides you through reflecting on your childhood — family dynamics, early experiences, messages you absorbed, and core beliefs that still influence you today. This is the first step in understanding what patterns were programmed before you had a choice.

## Output

Saves to: `my-brain/02_Wiki/campaign/01-childhood-patterns.md`

## Command Protocol

- **help** / **ask llm** — Get AI guidance on the current question
- **save** — Save progress and exit (resume later)
- **skip** — Skip current question
- **review** — Show all answers so far for editing
- **back** — Go to previous question
- **done** — Finalize and generate output

## Session Workflow

1. Introduce the session and explain the purpose
2. Ask questions one at a time, wait for the user's full answer before proceeding
3. After each answer, acknowledge and optionally offer a brief reflection
4. After all questions, show a review of all answers
5. Allow corrections via "I want to change answer X"
6. Once confirmed, write the output file to the vault
7. Mark skill as complete

## Questions

### Family of Origin

1. How would you describe your childhood in a few key memories or themes? What stands out most when you look back?

2. What roles did you play in your family? (e.g., caretaker, rebel, peacemaker, invisible one, golden child)

3. What patterns were modeled by your parents or caregivers — around work, money, conflict, love, rest?

4. Were there moments growing up where you felt unsafe, unseen, or not enough? What happened?

### Early Messages & Beliefs

5. What messages did you receive (directly or indirectly) from parents, teachers, or peers about who you should be?

6. Can you identify any early beliefs you absorbed? Examples: "I'm not good enough," "I must be perfect," "Success is dangerous," "I have to earn love," "Others are more important."

7. What did you learn about money from your family? What phrases or attitudes about money do you remember?

8. What did you learn about work and success? Was achievement celebrated, expected, ignored, or punished?

### Survival Mechanisms

9. How did you learn to cope with difficulty as a child? What strategies did you develop to feel safe, loved, or accepted?

10. Which of those childhood survival strategies are you still using today? Do they still serve you?

### Unresolved Past

11. Are there past situations or decisions you still replay or regret? What do you wish had gone differently?

12. If you could go back and tell your younger self one thing, what would it be?

### Integration

13. Looking at all of this now — what patterns from your childhood do you see still showing up in your adult life?

14. What's one belief or pattern from your early life that you're ready to examine more closely?
