# Goal: Obstacles, Skills & Resources

## What This Does

Uses Brian Tracy's steps 4-6 and the worksheet's pre-mortem approach to systematically identify what's in your way, what you need to learn, and whose help you need — then builds a plan to eliminate or work around each obstacle.

## Questions

### Step 1: Identify Obstacles

1. Why aren't you already at your goal? What's stopping you? List everything that comes to mind.

2. Internal obstacles: What within yourself is in the way? (Fear, doubt, limiting beliefs, lack of discipline, procrastination, perfectionism, imposter syndrome.)

3. External obstacles: What outside of you is in the way? (Money, time, location, responsibilities, health, lack of resources.)

4. The Theory of Constraints: There is always one limiting factor that sets the speed at which you achieve your goal. What is your #1 constraint right now?

5. Apply the 80/20 Rule: 80% of the reasons holding you back are inside yourself (lack of skill, knowledge, or quality). Only 20% are external. What internal obstacles are the real bottleneck?

### Step 2: Pre-Mortem

6. Imagine it's 5 years from now and you failed to achieve your goal. Be brutally honest: Why did you fail? Write down every reason.

7. Common failure reasons to consider: Got distracted by phone/social media. Watched too much entertainment. Didn't believe in myself. Quit when it got hard. Didn't follow the plan. Kept starting new goals instead of finishing one. Lost momentum. Burned out. Got overwhelmed.

8. For each potential failure reason, write: "How will I prevent this?" This is your first obstacle elimination system.

### Step 3: Knowledge & Skills Needed

9. What knowledge, information, or skills will you need to achieve your goal? List everything you need to learn or improve.

10. What one skill, if you developed it and did it in an excellent fashion, would have the greatest positive impact on your ability to reach your goal?

11. How will you acquire these skills? (Courses, books, mentors, practice, projects, coaching.)

12. What's your learning plan for the next 90 days?

### Step 4: People Whose Help You Need

13. List every person whose help, cooperation, or support you will need to achieve your goal — family, mentors, partners, team members, clients, supporters.

14. For each key person, ask: "What's in it for them?" How can you be a go-giver, not just a go-getter?

15. Who is one person who could open a door or make a decisive difference?

### Step 5: Obstacle Elimination Plan

16. For each major obstacle you identified, decide: Can I eliminate it? Avoid it? Reduce it? Work around it?

17. What are your "distraction shields"? (Apps blocked, workspace cleaned, phone on silent, specific time blocks.)

18. Create if-then fallbacks: "If X happens, then I will do Y." (e.g., "If I miss the gym, I will do 100 pushups at home.")

19. What's the #1 obstacle to solve first? Why?
