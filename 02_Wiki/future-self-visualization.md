# Future Self Visualization

A method for defining a compelling vision of your ideal future self — identity, character traits, habits, emotional baseline, and daily life.

## Summary

To create lasting change, you must define who you want to become. This is not fantasy but alignment: mapping the identity, habits, and mindset of your ideal self, then starting to show up as that person now, moment by moment.

## Key Claims

- The gap between current self and future self must not be too big — it can overwhelm and trigger self-sabotage (source: `01_Raw/transcript-future-self.md`)
- You need pitstops along the way (intermediate goals) to avoid getting scared by the distance (source: `01_Raw/transcript-future-self.md`)
- Define ideal character traits, values, habits — not just external goals (source: `01_Raw/worksheet-future-investigation.md`)

## Key Concepts

- **Identity & Self-Image**: How you describe the most aligned version of yourself
- **Core Values**: Principles this version lives by no matter what
- **Character Traits**: Qualities embodied even under pressure (calm, focused, honest, consistent)
- **Emotional Baseline**: How this future self feels most of the time
- **Inner Dialogue**: What replaces old self-criticism
- **Environmental Design**: The physical and digital environment that supports this version
