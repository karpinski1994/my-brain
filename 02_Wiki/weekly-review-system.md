# Weekly Review System

A structured reflection process to track progress, identify obstacles, and adjust your approach.

## Summary

Tracking progress is one of the biggest motivators for humans — the feeling of moving forward drives continued action. Weekly reviews help you see what you've actually done (not just results) and adjust your system. Focus on character change and action taken, not just physical results.

## Key Claims

- The feeling of progress is one of the biggest motivation drivers for humanity — like achievements in games (source: `01_Raw/transcript-forming-plan.md`)
- What matters is that you did it — not the outcome. Facing fear, overcoming obstacles, taking action is the clue (source: `01_Raw/transcript-forming-plan.md`)
- Results are side effects — if you keep doing things, results will start coming (source: `01_Raw/transcript-forming-plan.md`)

## Weekly Review Questions

- Did I follow my daily actions?
- What slowed me down?
- What was my biggest distraction?
- What was a win?
- What will I change next week?
- What will I eliminate to go faster?

## Principles

- Review every Sunday
- Be brutally honest — you can lie to anyone else but not yourself
- Focus on character and behavior change, not just outcomes
- If you never did something before and did it for the first time, that's a win regardless of outcome
- Progress without feedback = drifting
