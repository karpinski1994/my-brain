# Obstacle Elimination

A pre-mortem approach to identifying and eliminating obstacles before they derail your goals.

## Summary

Before you start executing a plan, imagine you've failed and work backwards to identify why. This reveals obstacles upfront so you can build systems that make failure nearly impossible. The goal is to design your environment and systems so there's no room for mistakes — only data gathering and improvement.

## Key Claims

- Pre-mortem: imagine you failed, write down why, then prevent each reason from happening (source: `01_Raw/transcript-forming-plan.md`)
- Once you know what obstacles will come (stress, fear, doubt, insecurity), you can prepare for them — they lose their power (source: `01_Raw/transcript-forming-plan.md`)
- Movement first, then adjustment — you can't steer a stationary car, you need data from real action to improve (source: `01_Raw/transcript-forming-plan.md`)
- Common failure reasons: distraction (phone, YouTube), lack of self-belief, quitting when hard, not following the plan, restarting new goals instead of finishing one (source: `01_Raw/worksheet-forming-plan.md`)

## Obstacle Elimination Strategies

- Distraction shields (blocked apps, clean workspace)
- Time blocks (no interruptions)
- If-then fallbacks ("If I miss the gym, I do 100 pushups at home")
- Prepare for known emotional states (stress, fear, doubt)
- Use AI tools (ChatGPT, Gemini) for personalized diagnosis and action plans
- Seek professional help when self-help isn't enough
