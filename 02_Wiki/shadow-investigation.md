# Shadow Investigation

A structured method for identifying and clearing the mental and emotional blocks (the "shadow") that hold you back from taking consistent action.

## Summary

Your past casts a shadow that determines how you think, feel, and act. Past experiences shape daily decisions and life results. To become unstoppable, you must first define and eliminate this shadow — self-limiting beliefs, traumas, self-sabotaging thoughts, and emotional baggage.

## Key Claims

- Your present behavior is deeply influenced by past experiences — childhood patterns, early messages, core beliefs (source: `01_Raw/transcript-investigating-past.md`)
- Awareness is the first step to change: identify the shadow before you can eliminate it (source: `01_Raw/worksheet-past-investigation.md`)
- Experts vs beginners: experts define what needs to be solved, beginners just implement; defining the shadow is the expert-level work (source: `01_Raw/transcript-investigating-past.md`)
- Not all issues need professional help — many can be resolved with self-investigation, AI tools (ChatGPT), and online resources (source: `01_Raw/transcript-investigating-past.md`)

## Key Concepts

- **Shadow**: All self-limiting beliefs, bad experiences, self-sabotaging thoughts, emotional baggage
- **Self-fulfilling prophecies**: Nourishing certain thoughts creates vicious circles
- **Emotional baggage**: Anger, shame, guilt, sadness that remains unresolved
- **Inner blocks**: Thoughts and fears that prevent action
- **Self-sabotage mechanisms**: The stories you tell yourself to justify inaction
