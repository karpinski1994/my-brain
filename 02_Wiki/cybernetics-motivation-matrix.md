# Cybernetics: Motivation Matrix

The complete framework of 12 motivation types, 6 stages of control, and the General Theory of Motivation — a rigorous, mathematical model of human and societal motivation.

## Summary

The control process has 6 sub-processes (3 cognitive + 3 decision), each splitting into 2 based on origin (internal/external or meta/sub), yielding 12 total motivation types. An autonomous system requires all 12 to function. Humanity (8 billion people) collectively fills all 12 slots.

## G-Potential and Correlation

- **G (Correlation Conductivity)**: Neural pathways through which power flows — like copper cable, built through repetition
- **K (Correlation Power)**: Actual energy flowing through G pathways — like electrical current
- Both must coexist for any cognitive process

### Three Correlation Paths
1. Reflexes
2. Consciousness
3. Imagination

## The Six Stages of Control

| Stage | Type | Question |
|-------|------|----------|
| Exploration | Cognitive | Does something exist? |
| Classification | Cognitive | What elements are there? |
| Explication | Cognitive | How are they connected? |
| Postulation | Decision | What should the goal be? |
| Optimization | Decision | What method should I use? |
| Realization | Decision | What resources do I need? |

## The 12 Motivation Types

Each of the 6 stages splits into 2:

**Cognitive (Meta/Sub):**
- Exploration of meta-systems vs sub-systems
- Classification of meta vs sub
- Explication of meta vs sub

**Decision (Internal/External):**
- Ideological vs Adaptive postulation
- Ethical vs Legal optimization
- Vital vs Economic realization

### Decision Role Matrix

Maps people by which decision stages they dominate:
- Investor, Entrepreneur, Cooperator, Solo Entrepreneur
- Advisor, Manager, Worker, Supervisory Board

## Key Insights

- Cognitive + Decision motivations = two legs — need both to function
- Confusing goals (postulation) with methods (optimization) is a common steering error
- Strategy = Postulation, Tactics = Realization, Operations = Optimization
- A person with strong decision but weak cognitive motivations builds utopias (disconnected from reality)
- A person with strong cognitive but weak decision motivations understands everything but never acts
- Ultimate driver of all motivation: **happiness** = alignment of character parameters with environment
