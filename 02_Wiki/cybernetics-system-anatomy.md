# Cybernetics: System Anatomy

The five functional organs of any autonomous system, and the hierarchy of system autonomy types.

## Summary

Every autonomous system has five functional organs operating on two parallel tracks (informational and energo-material). Systems are categorized by where the "Organizer" (goal-setter) resides: Steerable (external), Self-Steering (external goals, internal operations), or Autonomous (internal Homeostat).

## The Five Functional Organs

1. **Receptor** — gathers input from the environment (sensory)
2. **Correlator** — processes information, makes decisions, generates emotions (brain + nervous + hormonal)
3. **Supplier** — gathers energy/matter from environment
4. **Accumulator** — stores and distributes energy/potential (blood, battery)
5. **Homeostat** — the internal organizer; sets goals, defends equilibrium. The "soul" of the system.

### Two Parallel Tracks
- **Informational Track**: Receptor → Correlator (processes data)
- **Energo-Material Track**: Supplier → Accumulator (processes energy)
- **Effector**: Shared output organ combining both tracks

## System Hierarchy

| Type | Organizer | Example | Behavior |
|------|-----------|---------|----------|
| Steerable | External | Flashlight, micromanaged business | No internal decision-making |
| Self-Steering | External | AI assistant, corporate department | Manages operations but follows external goals |
| Autonomous | Internal (Homeostat) | Human, self-sustaining organization | Defines own goals, defends existence |

## The Correlator (Details)

- Four internal potentials: Receptor, Homeostat (reflective), Estimatory (decision output), Perturbatory (emotional output)
- Outputs: **Decisions** (actions) and **Emotions** (signals to Homeostat about equilibrium)
- **Rigid Parameters**: Intelligence (quantity of nodes), Aptitude (speed), Talent (uneven distribution of dense connections)

## The Homeostat (Details)

- The fifth organ — source of **goal-setting** and **counter-threat capability**
- Mathematical reactivity: -1 (inverts threats to restore equilibrium)
- Four connections: Emotion from Correlator, Reflection to Correlator, Load from Accumulator, Tension to Accumulator
- Defines what makes a system alive — self-defined goals and biological reproduction

## Business Application

Founder = Homeostat (defines WHY). CEO/Managers = Correlators (executes HOW). Founders should work ON the business, not IN it.
